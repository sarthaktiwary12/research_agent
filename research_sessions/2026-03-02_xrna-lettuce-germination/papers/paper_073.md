## Abstract
Members of the mitochondrial transcription termination factor (mTERF) family are found in metazoans and plants and regulate organellar gene expression at multiple levels. While metazoans have 3-4 mTERF members, plant genomes encode more than 30 (35 in Arabidopsis), most targeted to mitochondria and/or chloroplasts. In chloroplasts, specific mTERFs have been experimentally shown to function in: transcription termination (mTERF6 for rpoA; mTERF8 for psbJ); transcriptional pausing and transcript stabilization (MDA1/mTERF5 for psbE and ndhA); intron splicing (BSM/RUG2/mTERF4 and Zm-mTERF4 for group II introns); and ribosome assembly and translation (mTERF9/TWIRT1). mTERF6 binds specifically to the chloroplast trnI.2 DNA target site and can terminate transcription in vitro, while also being required for trnI.2 maturation. mTERF2 promotes splicing of group IIB introns. The RHON1 protein also participates in chloroplast transcription termination regulation. These factors are critical for proper chloroplast gene expression, with the unique hybrid transcription system of chloroplasts consisting of two types of RNA polymerases of dissimilar phylogenetic origin.
