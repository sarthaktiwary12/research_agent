## Abstract
N6-methyladenine (m6A) is the most abundant and reversible chemical modification in eukaryotic mRNA, and as an epitranscriptomic mark it regulates plant development and stress response. This study found that the expression of ALKBH10B is increased in response to abscisic acid (ABA), osmotic, and salt stress. The alkbh10b mutants showed hypersensitivity to ABA, osmotic, and salt stress during seed germination. Transcriptome analysis revealed that the expression of several ABA response genes is upregulated in alkbh10b-1 compared to wild type. m6A sequencing showed that ABA signaling genes, including PYR1, PYL7, PYL9, ABI1, and SnRK2.2, are m6A hypermethylated in alkbh10b-1 after ABA treatment. This demonstrates a direct crosstalk between m6A epitranscriptomics and ABA signaling in seed germination, where the m6A eraser ALKBH10B removes m6A marks from core ABA pathway transcripts to modulate ABA sensitivity during germination.
