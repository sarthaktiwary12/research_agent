## Abstract
Selected stress-induced 5'-tiRNAs inhibit protein synthesis by displacing eIF4G/eIF4A from uncapped and capped RNAs, and also displace eIF4F (but not eIF4E:4EBP1) from isolated m7G cap. The authors identified a terminal oligoguanine (TOG) motif at the 5' end of specific tiRNAs that is required to displace the eIF4F complex, inhibit translation, and induce stress granule assembly. Specific 5'-tiRNAs from tRNA-Ala and tRNA-Cys show translation repression dependent on five consecutive guanosines at the 5' termini (5'-TOG motif). The 5' half of tRNA-Ala with 5'-TOG was a potent inhibitor, whereas the 5' half of tRNA-Met, devoid of this motif, did not repress translation despite the same predicted secondary structure.
