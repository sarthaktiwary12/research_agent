## Abstract
Reactive oxygen species (ROS) are increasingly recognized as non-enzymatic players in radicle elongation growth and endosperm weakening during seed germination. NADPH oxidases (Rbohs) are key enzymes for ROS production. One lettuce Rboh gene, LsRbohC1, was cloned, and over-expression and RNAi lines were generated. LsRbohC1 was abundantly expressed in germinating seeds, especially in the endosperm cap and hypocotyl. Over-expression/knock-down of LsRbohC1 increased/decreased the production of O2·- in the radicle and endosperm cap, demonstrating a direct causal role for NADPH oxidase-generated ROS in lettuce seed germination.
