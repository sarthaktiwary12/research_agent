## Abstract
Recent investigations document that plants can uptake and process externally applied double-stranded RNAs (dsRNAs), hairpin RNAs (hpRNAs), and small interfering RNAs (siRNAs) designed to silence important genes of plant pathogenic viruses, fungi, or insects. The exogenously applied RNAs spread locally and systemically, move into the pathogens, and induce RNA interference-mediated plant pathogen resistance. Available application methods include spraying, mechanical inoculation, root or seed soaking, foliar application, infiltration, and trunk injections. Artificial RNAs can be combined with cationic nanoparticles, clay nanosheets, surfactants, and peptide-based RNA delivery systems to increase uptake.
