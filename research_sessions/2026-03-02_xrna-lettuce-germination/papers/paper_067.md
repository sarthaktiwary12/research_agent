## Abstract
TEN1, a component of the CTC1/STN1/TEN1 (CST) complex essential for plant and vertebrate telomeres, is encoded by the previously characterized gene MERISTEM DISORGANIZATION1 (MDO1). A point mutation (G77E) in MDO1 triggers stem cell differentiation and death, constitutive DNA damage response, frequent telomere fusions, increased single-strand telomeric DNA, and telomeric circles. Unlike stn1 or ctc1 mutants, telomerase enzyme activity is elevated in ten1-3 mutants due to increased repeat addition processivity, indicating TEN1 plays an unexpected role in modulating telomerase activity. First-generation ten1-3 mutants are smaller with smaller leaves, fused stems, and irregular phyllotaxy. Second-generation mutants display more severe growth phenotypes, shoot apical meristem abnormalities, and fail to produce true leaves. This study directly links telomere protection via the CST complex to plant meristem function, stem cell maintenance, and development in Arabidopsis.
