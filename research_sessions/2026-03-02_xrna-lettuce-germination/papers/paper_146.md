## Key Findings

### Seed Priming Molecular Phases
- **Phase I** (priming treatment): DNA/RNA repair, mitochondrial repair, respiration activation, ROS signaling, antioxidant defense, cell cycle initiation, stress response gene induction (dehydrin, aquaporin, hormone signaling)
- **Phase II** (germination after re-hydration): Priming memory recruited; proteins synthesized from new mRNA
- **Phase III** (post-germination): Storage reserve mobilization, radicle cell elongation, seed coat rupture

### RNA Repair During Priming/Imbibition
- DNA and RNA repair are major biochemical processes during seed imbibition
- Isoaspartate formation (from oxidative damage) is repaired by isoaspartyl methyltransferase
- This repair enzyme is active during imbibition, particularly on proteins involved in **ribosomal RNA processing in the nucleolus**
- **Connection to NUC1**: Nucleolin is the most abundant nucleolar protein involved in rRNA processing — its function during seed imbibition would be critical for ribosome assembly

### Stored mRNAs and Early Germination
- De novo protein synthesis required for germination (cycloheximide blocks germination in lettuce, wheat, Arabidopsis, rice)
- Stored mRNAs (long-lived mRNAs) provide templates for Phase I protein synthesis
- No correlation between transcript and protein levels during germination (r^2 = 0.02)
- This means post-transcriptional regulation dominates early germination

### RNA Synthesis in Lettuce Specifically
- RNA synthesis measured during lettuce imbibition as 32P incorporation
- Much higher rate of RNA synthesis in illuminated (germinating) vs. dark (non-germinating) seeds
- Difference develops only after 12h of imbibition at 27C (onset of germination)
- Before 12h: translation of stored mRNAs dominates
- After 12h: new transcription becomes important

### Priming Effects on Gene Expression
- DSB repair important for seed longevity after priming
- Brassinosteroid biosynthesis/signaling genes upregulated in primed seeds with shorter longevity
- Cell wall modification genes highly expressed in primed seeds

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- **Timing window for xRNA application**:
  - 0-12h: xRNA would primarily affect translation of stored mRNAs (post-transcriptional)
  - 12h+: xRNA could also affect newly transcribed mRNAs
- **RNA repair**: Seeds actively repair damaged RNA during imbibition. xRNA stability could benefit from this repair capacity, or could be degraded by quality control mechanisms
- **Nucleolar function**: rRNA processing (involving nucleolin/NUC1) is critical during imbibition for ribosome assembly — connects to RGG and Pol I targets
- **The priming paradigm**: Seed priming (controlled hydration-dehydration) enhances germination by activating repair and stress response pathways. xRNA treatment during priming could be a practical delivery strategy

## Limitations
- Review article — no original lettuce transcriptomic data
- Priming-specific molecular changes may not fully represent normal germination
- Lettuce RNA synthesis data from old studies (1960s-70s) with limited resolution
