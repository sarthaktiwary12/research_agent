## Key Findings

### PP2C Family in Sunflower (Asteraceae, same family as lettuce)
- 121 PP2C genes identified in the sunflower genome (HanPP2C1 to HanPP2C121)
- Distributed unevenly across 17 chromosomes
- All contain the Type-2C phosphatase domain

### Phylogenetic Classification
- Constructed phylogenetic tree using 121 HanPP2C + 80 AtPP2C proteins
- HanPP2C genes categorized into **15 subgroups** (A-L, with A, B, and F further split into A1, A2, B1, B2, F1, F2)
- 7 HanPP2C genes not clustered with any subgroup
- **Subgroup D** was the largest (20 HanPP2Cs)
- **Subgroup G** was second largest (15 HanPP2Cs)

### Subgroup Sizes in Sunflower
- A: 8 genes | B: 9 genes | C: 7 genes | D: 20 genes | E: 7 genes
- F: 14 genes | G: 15 genes | H: 8 genes | I: 5 genes | J: 8 genes
- K: 4 genes | L: 2 genes

### Gene Duplication
- 48 of 53 HanPP2C gene pairs showed segmental duplication (major expansion mechanism)
- Only 5 pairs showed tandem duplication
- Strong purifying selection pressure

### Subcellular Localization
- Most HanPP2C proteins localized to nucleus, cytoplasm, and chloroplast

### Stress Response
- 71 miRNAs targeting 86 HanPP2C genes identified
- Expression profiling under drought, salt, heat, and cold stress

## Critical Relevance to Lettuce PP2C47
- Sunflower is the closest published PP2C family study to lettuce (both Asteraceae)
- The 15-subgroup classification (A-L) framework applies to lettuce
- With 121 PP2C genes in sunflower, lettuce likely has a similar number
- The lettuce PP2C47 target could be mapped by BLAST against the sunflower PP2C family
- If "PP2C47" numbering follows chromosomal order (as in other species), it could fall in any clade

## Limitations
- Sunflower is Heliantheae tribe; lettuce is Cichorieae tribe — significant divergence within Asteraceae
- No functional validation of individual HanPP2C genes
- Gene numbering is species-specific; sunflower HanPP2C47 is NOT necessarily homologous to lettuce PP2C47
