## Abstract
Weakening of the endosperm during seed germination is inseparable from the relaxation of the endosperm cell walls by hydrolytic enzymes. Xyloglucan endotransglucosylase/hydrolases (XTHs) are key cell wall-modifying enzymes. Transcriptome analysis of the endosperm cap detected expression of 10 LsXTH genes, among which LsXTH43 exhibited the highest expression during germination and was significantly upregulated two-fold by high temperatures, suggesting a potential role in high-temperature germination. This is the first comprehensive identification and functional analysis of the XTH gene family in lettuce seeds.
