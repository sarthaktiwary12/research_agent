## Abstract
Comprehensive review of lettuce thermoinhibition mechanisms. Endo-beta-mannanase is crucial for endosperm weakening prior to radicle emergence and is a pivotal factor for germination at high temperatures. LsMAN1 is induced by GA and inhibited by ABA; its mRNA abundance is high in both temperature-sensitive and temperature-tolerant genotypes immediately after radicle emergence at 20C, but expression is low under heat stress at 35C. For lettuce seeds, both temporary germination inhibition (thermoinhibition) and permanent inhibition are caused by hardening of the endosperm, preventing normal radicle protrusion. LsNCED4 upregulation drives ABA accumulation. Light (phytochrome) modulates both ABA and GA levels. Wild L. serriola shows relaxed thermoinhibition.
