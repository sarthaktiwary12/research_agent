# Abscisic acid in the thermoinhibition of lettuce seed germination and enhancement of its catabolism by gibberellin

## Abstract
This seminal study demonstrates that ABA is a key factor in lettuce seed thermoinhibition and that GA affects temperature responsiveness through ABA metabolism. ABA contents were elevated in Salinas lettuce seeds exhibiting thermoinhibition, consistent with the ability of fluridone (ABA biosynthesis inhibitor) to improve germination at high temperatures. At 28 degrees C, thermoinhibition was prevented by fluridone application. At 33 degrees C, seed sensitivity to ABA increased and fluridone alone was no longer effective — but combined fluridone + GA3 treatment was effective, as GA3 promoted the decomposition of ABA. This demonstrates a dual mechanism: GA counteracts ABA-mediated thermoinhibition both by reducing ABA levels (via enhanced catabolism) and independently promoting germination. The study established the fundamental ABA-GA antagonism model for lettuce thermoinhibition that has guided subsequent research.

### CRITICAL ANALYSIS: Temperature-Dependent ABA Sensitivity and PP2C47

**The most important finding for the PP2C47 question is claim #3: ABA SENSITIVITY INCREASES at higher temperatures.** This means:

- At 33 degrees C, even when ABA biosynthesis is blocked (fluridone), seeds are still inhibited because they become MORE SENSITIVE to whatever ABA remains
- This temperature-dependent ABA sensitivity change likely operates DOWNSTREAM of ABA biosynthesis — at the level of ABA signaling components including PP2Cs and SnRK2s

**For PP2C47 targeting in lettuce:**
- If PP2C47 is a clade A PP2C, its downregulation would FURTHER increase ABA sensitivity
- Combined with the natural increase in ABA sensitivity at high temperature, PP2C47 downregulation would compound the thermoinhibition problem
- The fact that fluridone alone fails at 33 degrees C (even though it blocks NEW ABA synthesis) shows that the ABA signaling pathway sensitivity itself is the limiting factor at extreme temperatures — precisely the pathway that PP2C47 regulates

**This paper provides the quantitative physiological context:** The 28 degrees C vs. 33 degrees C threshold shows that ABA signaling sensitivity is tuned to a narrow temperature range. Even small changes in ABA signaling sensitivity (such as PP2C downregulation) could shift seeds from the "germinable" to "thermoinhibited" zone.
