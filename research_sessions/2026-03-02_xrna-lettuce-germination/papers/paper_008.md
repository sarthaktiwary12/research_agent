## Abstract
This seminal study demonstrates that ABA is a key factor in lettuce seed thermoinhibition and that GA affects temperature responsiveness through ABA metabolism. ABA contents were elevated in Salinas lettuce seeds exhibiting thermoinhibition, consistent with the ability of fluridone (ABA biosynthesis inhibitor) to improve germination at high temperatures. At 28 degrees C, thermoinhibition was prevented by fluridone application. At 33 degrees C, seed sensitivity to ABA increased and fluridone alone was no longer effective — but combined fluridone + GA3 treatment was effective, as GA3 promoted the decomposition of ABA. This demonstrates a dual mechanism: GA counteracts ABA-mediated thermoinhibition both by reducing ABA levels (via enhanced catabolism) and independently promoting germination. The study established the fundamental ABA-GA antagonism model for lettuce thermoinhibition that has guided subsequent research.
