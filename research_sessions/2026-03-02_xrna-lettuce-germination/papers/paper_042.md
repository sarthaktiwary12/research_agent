## Abstract
Some transfer RNA-derived fragments (tRFs) produced by the rhizobium Bradyrhizobium japonicum can silence genes in the soybean Glycine max to promote nodulation. Plant and mammalian tRNA-derived sRNAs (tsRNAs) are commonly retrieved in extracellular compartments and biofluids, and have recently been shown to be transferred bidirectionally between host and microbes. The study identified that some of the top over-represented sRNA sequences internalized by Pseudomonas syringae are tRFs, derived from various tRNA precursors. The APF fraction co-purifying with TET8-positive extracellular vesicles (EVs) contains active antibacterial sRNAs, and the authors also identified a novel class of active sRNAs unbound to proteins in the supernatant fraction, termed "extracellular free sRNAs" (efsRNAs), sensitive to MNase action.

## Key Findings
- tRNA-derived fragments (tRFs) from rhizobia can silence genes in soybean to promote nodulation
- Plant tRNA-derived sRNAs are found in extracellular compartments and can transfer bidirectionally between hosts and microbes
- Top internalized sRNAs by P. syringae include tRFs from various tRNA precursors
- TET8-positive EVs contain active antibacterial sRNAs
- Novel class "extracellular free sRNAs" (efsRNAs) identified — non-vesicular, non-protein-bound, active in gene silencing
