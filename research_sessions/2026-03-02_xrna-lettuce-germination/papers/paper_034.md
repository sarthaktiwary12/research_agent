## Abstract
Thioredoxins (Trx) and glutaredoxins (Grx) constitute families of thiol oxidoreductases, and knowledge of Trx and Grx in plants has dramatically increased during the last decade. The isolation of mutants inactivated in multiple genes and several genetic screens allowed the demonstration of the involvement of Trx and Grx in pathogen response, phytohormone pathways, and at several control points of plant development. Glutaredoxins play central roles in redox homeostasis and signaling, and in iron metabolism in eukaryotes. Class-1 glutaredoxins are characterized as cytosolic GSH-dependent oxidoreductases that catalyze reversible protein S-glutathionylation, contributing to the regulation of redox signal transduction and protection of protein thiols from irreversible oxidation.

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** Research question 5 — glutaredoxin/thioredoxin redox signaling during the seed-to-seedling transition. Provides the mechanistic framework for understanding what glutaredoxin downregulation would do to redox homeostasis.
- **Partially addresses:** The connection between Grx and "phytohormone pathways" is relevant to question 1 (ABA/GA balance), as ABA signaling involves redox-sensitive components. The "iron metabolism" role of Grx connects to question 5 regarding Fe-S cluster biology (HCF101 context).
- **Novel contribution:** The key insight is the DUAL role of glutaredoxins: (1) redox signal transduction via S-glutathionylation, and (2) iron-sulfur cluster transfer/sensing. This means glutaredoxin downregulation during germination would simultaneously affect: (a) redox signaling cascades that control the metabolic transition, (b) Fe-S cluster delivery to proteins like those assembled by HCF101, and (c) protection against irreversible thiol oxidation during the oxidative burst of imbibition. This creates a mechanistic link between the "redox kick-start" (paper_093) and the "Fe-S cluster assembly" (paper_026) themes.

### Connections to Other Session Papers
- Directly connects to paper_026 (HCF101): Glutaredoxins are involved in Fe-S cluster transfer — particularly monothiol Grx (Class-2) serve as scaffold/carrier proteins for [2Fe-2S] clusters. HCF101 assembles [4Fe-4S] clusters, and the upstream Fe-S assembly pathway requires Grx for initial cluster formation on scaffold proteins. Downregulating both Grx and HCF101 would create a double bottleneck in chloroplast Fe-S protein maturation.
- Directly connects to paper_093 (redox kick-start): The "thiol switching" described in the redox kick-start model is precisely the domain of Trx and Grx. The Cys oxidation states that control metabolic enzyme activity during germination are regulated by Trx/Grx systems. Grx downregulation would alter the kinetics of the redox kick-start by slowing the reduction of oxidized protein thiols during imbibition.
- Connects to paper_033 (TIM23): Mitochondrial Grx (e.g., GRXS15) must be imported via TIM23. If TIM23-2 is downregulated, mitochondrial Grx import could be delayed, compounding the effect of Grx transcript-level downregulation.
- Connects to paper_073 (mTERF): Some chloroplast gene expression factors are redox-regulated. Trx/Grx modulation could indirectly affect mTERF function if these proteins contain redox-sensitive Cys residues.
- Connects to paper_075 (PPO): PPO generates reactive quinones that can deplete cellular GSH (glutathione), which is the electron donor for Grx. If PPO is downregulated, GSH pools may be better preserved, partially compensating for Grx downregulation.
