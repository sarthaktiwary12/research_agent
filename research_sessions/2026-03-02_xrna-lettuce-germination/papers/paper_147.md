## Key Findings

### Plant Nucleolin Is Exclusively Nuclear (NO Cell-Surface Localization)
- **Immunogold electron microscopy** (Pontvianne et al., 2007): Gold particles detected EXCLUSIVELY in nucleus and nucleolus
- **NO gold particles detected in cytoplasm or organelles**
- AtNUC-L1 localizes mainly in the dense fibrillar component (DFC) of the nucleolus
- Some labeling in non-nucleolar regions of the nucleus
- No labeling in fibrillar centers (FCs), granular component (GC), or nucleolar vacuole

### This Directly Contradicts the Animal Nucleolin Model
- Animal nucleolin: found in nucleolus, nucleoplasm, cytoplasm, AND cell membrane
- Animal cell-surface nucleolin: mediates extracellular nucleic acid uptake
- Plant nucleolin (AtNUC-L1): **nucleus-only localization** — NO cell-surface access
- **The nucleolin-mediated xRNA uptake model from animal cells DOES NOT APPLY to plants**

### Two Plant Nucleolin Genes: NUC1 and NUC2
- Located on chromosomes 1 and 3 (duplicated regions) in Arabidopsis
- NUC1 (AT1G48920): Ubiquitously expressed, essential for nucleolar organization
- NUC2 (AT3G18610): Not expressed in normal conditions; activated in nuc1 mutant background
- nuc1 nuc2 double mutant is LETHAL — nucleolins are essential

### NUC1 Functions
- Ribosome biogenesis (rRNA processing)
- Nucleolar organization (dense fibrillar component)
- rDNA chromatin regulation (DNA methylation, variant expression)
- Nucleosome remodeling (in vitro)
- Telomere biology
- Proteasome dynamics

### NUC2 Functions (Antagonistic to NUC1)
- Controls rDNA variant expression through different mechanism
- Induces CG hypermethylation of rDNA when disrupted
- Regulates flowering time via FLC expression
- Binds "silenced" chromatin; participates in heterochromatin establishment

### Structural Differences
- NUC1: 45/60 R/G residues in C-terminal region; two bipartite NLSs
- NUC2: 26/59 R/G residues; one NLS
- Reduced R/G domain in NUC2 prevents proper subnucleolar compartment establishment

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- **RESOLVES Contradiction 9 (Nucleolin Uptake)**: Plant nucleolin is NOT on the cell surface
- The xRNA drug CANNOT enter plant cells via nucleolin-mediated endocytosis
- Alternative uptake pathways must be invoked:
  1. Passive diffusion through seed coat during imbibition (seed coat permeability)
  2. Symplastic entry through damaged cells in seed coat
  3. Non-nucleolin receptor-mediated endocytosis
  4. Direct uptake through cell wall pores (small RNA size may allow this)
- If the xRNA RGG target is nucleolin itself, then downregulating NUC1 would severely impair ribosome biogenesis during germination — likely ANTI-germination

## Limitations
- Only Arabidopsis studied; lettuce NUC1/NUC2 not characterized
- Immunogold EM is definitive for localization but only captures steady-state
- Cannot completely rule out transient cytoplasmic shuttling not captured by EM
- However, the contrast with animal nucleolin is clear and consistent across multiple studies
