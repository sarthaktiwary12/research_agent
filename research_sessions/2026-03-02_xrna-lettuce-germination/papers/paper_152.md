## Key Findings

### Dynamic miRNA Expression During Imbibition
- 58 miRNAs from 30 families identified at different germination stages
- 15 miRNAs and their targets significantly differentially expressed between:
  - Dry seeds
  - 12h imbibition
  - 24h imbibition
  - 48h imbibition
- Imbibition and temperature dynamically regulate developmentally important miRNAs

### miR390-TAS3-ARF Module in Germination
- miR390 triggers ta-siRNA production from TAS3 locus
- TAS3-derived ta-siRNAs target ARF2, ARF3, ARF4 (auxin response factors)
- This module is important for seed germination
- Represents a multi-layer small RNA regulatory cascade

### siRNA Characteristics During Germination
- ~46% of siRNAs were 21 nucleotides
- ~54% were 22 nucleotides
- siRNA targets: cytochrome complex function, root development, cell maturation, carbohydrate metabolism
- siRNAs remained relatively stable even in low-viability seeds
- Suggests siRNAs maintain seed metabolic activity during dormancy release

### DCL1-ABA Connection (from related studies)
- absg1 and absg2 (ABA supersensitive germination mutants) are alleles of DCL1 and HEN1
- DCL1/HEN1 deficiency upregulates ABA-responsive genes
- Small RNA biogenesis is directly connected to ABA sensitivity during germination

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- The germination small RNA landscape is complex and temporally regulated
- xRNA tRFs would be introduced into a system with active endogenous small RNA regulation
- The miR390-TAS3-ARF module shows that small RNAs regulate auxin signaling during germination
- Auxin is one of the hormones affected by UVR8 downregulation (paper_022 et al.)
- siRNA stability even in aged seeds suggests that the RNAi machinery persists through dormancy
- 21-22 nt siRNAs are the predominant size class — xRNA tRFs should be designed in this range

## Limitations
- Arabidopsis data, not lettuce-specific
- Only 15 miRNAs showed significant differential expression
- ta-siRNA-ARF module characterized but not all targets validated
