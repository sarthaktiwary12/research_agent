# Identification, Expression, and Import of Components 17 and 23 of the Inner Mitochondrial Membrane Translocase from Arabidopsis

- **Year:** 2003
- **Source:** Plant Physiology

## Abstract
All Arabidopsis TIM23 predicted proteins appeared to lack the first 34 amino acids compared with yeast TIM23. All AtTIM17 and AtTIM23 genes were expressed but displayed different tissue and developmental profiles. Import assays with AtTIM17 and AtTIM23 indicated that they both contained internal signals for integration into the inner mitochondrial membrane in a membrane potential-dependent manner. The novel extension at the C terminus of AtTIM17-2 links the outer and inner membrane in a manner analogous to yeast TIM23. Plant mitochondria have several unique features, such as a larger genome and a branched electron transport pathway, and are also involved in additional cellular functions such as photorespiration and stress perception.

### Key Findings
- Arabidopsis TIM23 proteins lack the first 34 amino acids compared to yeast TIM23, indicating structural divergence in plant mitochondrial import machinery
- Multiple AtTIM17 and AtTIM23 gene family members are expressed with different tissue and developmental profiles, suggesting functional specialization
- Both AtTIM17 and AtTIM23 contain internal (not N-terminal) signals for integration into the inner mitochondrial membrane
- Integration is membrane potential (delta-psi) dependent, consistent with the electrochemical requirement for TIM-mediated import
- AtTIM17-2 has a novel C-terminal extension that links outer and inner mitochondrial membranes, analogous to the N-terminal domain of yeast TIM23 — this is a plant-specific adaptation
- Plant mitochondria have unique features including a larger genome, branched electron transport (alternative oxidase, alternative NAD(P)H dehydrogenases), and roles in photorespiration and stress perception
