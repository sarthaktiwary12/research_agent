## Abstract
This study analyzes comparative genetics, physiology, and gene expression between thermosensitive Lactuca sativa 'Salinas' (fails to germinate above 25-30 degrees C) and thermotolerant L. serriola UC96US23 (germinates up to 37 degrees C in light). Germination of the two genotypes was differentially sensitive to ABA and GA at elevated temperatures. ABA levels in imbibed Salinas seeds were almost 5-fold greater than those in UC96US23. A major QTL (Htg6.1) from UC96US23 confers germination thermotolerance, and LsNCED4, an ABA biosynthetic gene, maps to the center of this QTL interval. The sensitivity of lettuce seed germination to ABA inhibition is enhanced at higher temperatures. Exposure to ethylene, GA, and red light can increase the upper temperature limit of germination. The study provides a detailed molecular basis for natural genetic variation in lettuce seed thermoinhibition, linking it to differential expression of hormone biosynthesis, metabolism, and response genes.

### CRITICAL ANALYSIS: The ABA Sensitivity Enhancement at High Temperature

**This paper provides a critical quantitative framework for the PP2C47 question:**

The 5-fold difference in ABA levels between thermosensitive Salinas and thermotolerant UC96US23 demonstrates that ABA accumulation is the PRIMARY driver of thermoinhibition. But crucially, the paper also confirms that ABA SENSITIVITY (not just level) increases at high temperature.

**For PP2C47 in xRNA context:**

The ABA sensitivity enhancement at high temperature likely involves changes in the PP2C-SnRK2 signaling module. Several possible mechanisms:
1. PP2C expression may decrease at high temperature (less negative regulation → more ABA sensitivity)
2. SnRK2 expression may increase at high temperature
3. PYR/PYL receptor sensitivity to ABA may increase
4. Post-translational modifications may shift PP2C-SnRK2 balance

If mechanism #1 is correct (PP2C expression naturally decreases at high temperature to increase ABA sensitivity), then:
- The plant is ALREADY downregulating PP2Cs at high temperature as part of the thermoinhibition response
- xRNA-mediated PP2C47 downregulation would AMPLIFY this natural thermoinhibition mechanism
- This makes PP2C47 an especially counterproductive target for promoting germination under heat stress

**Natural variation angle:** The thermotolerant wild lettuce (L. serriola) likely has differences in ABA signaling sensitivity, possibly including PP2C expression levels. If thermotolerant genotypes have HIGHER PP2C expression (less ABA sensitivity), this would further confirm that PP2C downregulation is counterproductive.

### Limitations
- **Stated by authors:** Not accessible from abstract

The QTL analysis identifies the major-effect locus but there are likely minor-effect QTLs. Gene expression analysis at specific timepoints may miss dynamic regulation. Published in 2008 — before CRISPR and modern single-cell transcriptomics.
