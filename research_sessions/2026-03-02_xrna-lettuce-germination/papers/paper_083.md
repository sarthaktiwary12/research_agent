## Abstract
ABA induces dormancy during seed maturation and maintains dormancy in mature seeds, whereas GA stimulates germination and has been implicated in dormancy loss through after-ripening and cold stratification. The antagonistic crosstalk between gibberellic acid (GA) and abscisic acid (ABA) plays a pivotal role in the modulation of seed germination. Key ABA signaling genes ABI3, ABI4, and ABI5 play important multiple functions during seed development including de-greening, desiccation tolerance, maturation, dormancy, and seed vigor. ABI4 positively regulates primary seed dormancy by downregulating GA biosynthesis and by inhibiting ABA catabolic genes (CYP707A1 and CYP707A2). In lettuce, ABA levels are controlled by the downregulation of LsNCED2 and LsNCED4 expression and upregulation of LsABA8ox4. High temperature induces ABA biosynthesis genes (ZEP, NCED2, NCED5, and NCED9) and increases ABA levels whereas it decreases GA levels by repressing GA biosynthesis genes in Arabidopsis seeds.
