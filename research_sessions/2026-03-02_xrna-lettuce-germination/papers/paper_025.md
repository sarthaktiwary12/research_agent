# JMJ24 Antagonizes Histone H3K9 Demethylase IBM1/JMJ25 Function and Interacts with RNAi Pathways for Gene Silencing

## Metadata

## Abstract
Removal of H3K9me2 from gene bodies by the jmjC histone demethylase IBM1/JMJ25 inhibits DNA methylation and derepresses gene expression. This study analyzed the function of a closely related homolog of IBM1/JMJ25, namely JMJ24. JMJ24 antagonizes IBM1/JMJ25 function and interacts with RNAi pathways for gene silencing. The balance between JMJ24 and JMJ25 activities controls the chromatin state at specific gene loci, regulating the interplay between histone demethylation, DNA methylation, and gene silencing pathways.

## Deep Reading Notes

> **Note: Analysis based on abstract only. Evidence confidence is reduced.**

### Key Findings
- JMJ25 (also called IBM1, INCREASE IN BONSAI METHYLATION 1) is a jmjC domain-containing histone demethylase that specifically removes H3K9me2 (dimethylation of histone H3 lysine 9) and H3K9me1 from gene bodies in Arabidopsis
- IBM1/JMJ25 belongs to the KDM3/JHDM2 subfamily of demethylases; it prevents ectopic H3K9me2 accumulation in gene bodies, thereby protecting genes from CHG DNA hypermethylation via the CMT3-KYP self-reinforcing loop
- JMJ24 is a closely related homolog of IBM1/JMJ25 but LACKS H3K9me2 demethylase activity (missing conserved cofactor binding sites); instead, JMJ24 has evolved a different function
- JMJ24 ANTAGONIZES IBM1/JMJ25 function: jmj24 mutation can complement (rescue) the growth defects and gene expression changes caused by ibm1 mutation
- JMJ24 interacts with RNAi pathways for gene silencing — jmj24-3 synergizes with dcl2/3/4 triple mutations to impair plant development
- The balance between JMJ24 and JMJ25 activities controls the chromatin state at specific gene loci
- JMJ24 possesses ubiquitin E3 ligase activity and directly targets the DNA methyltransferase CMT3 for proteasomal degradation (from related 2016 Deng et al. study)
- In ibm1/jmj25 mutants: H3K9me2 levels are distinctly increased in gene bodies while H3K4me3 (activation mark) is reduced in an antagonistic manner; this affects expression of genes including RDR2 and DCL3
- IBM1/JMJ25 is required for defense gene expression: bacteria-induced up-regulation of PR1, PR2, and FRK1 is abolished in ibm1 mutants due to enrichment of H3K9me2 and reduction of H3K4me3 at these loci
- IBM1's own expression is regulated by an epigenetic sensor: DNA methylation within its large 7th intron is required for production of the functional IBM1-L isoform (vs. non-functional IBM1-S)
- jmj24 mutations produce only subtle developmental defects, affecting relatively few genes at the vegetative stage

### Limitations

- **Additional concerns:** (1) The study does not address germination or seed development at all — the focus is on vegetative chromatin regulation. (2) JMJ24 produces only "subtle developmental defects," making its biological significance during germination uncertain. (3) The paper title mentions JMJ25, but it is primarily about JMJ24's role in antagonizing JMJ25 — direct germination-specific roles of JMJ25 are not characterized here. (4) Extrapolation from Arabidopsis to lettuce requires caution, especially for fine-tuned chromatin regulation. (5) No data on what happens when JMJ25 is specifically downregulated during germination.

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** Research Question 4 — "How do virilizer (m6A), JMJ25 demethylase, RGG RNA-binding protein, and Pol I/Pol beta subunits affect transcriptomic reprogramming during germination?" This paper provides the mechanistic framework for JMJ25 function: it removes H3K9me2 from gene bodies to prevent ectopic gene silencing.
- **Partially addresses:** The question of what happens when JMJ25 is downregulated. Based on this paper's evidence: JMJ25 downregulation would lead to increased H3K9me2 at gene bodies, increased CHG methylation, and ectopic gene silencing. For germination, this could mean: (a) silencing of genes that would otherwise be expressed during germination — potentially deleterious; or (b) silencing of stress/defense genes whose expression during germination is costly — potentially beneficial for resource reallocation. The paper's finding that IBM1/JMJ25 is required for defense gene activation (PR1, PR2) suggests its downregulation could suppress defense transcripts, consistent with the "defense reallocation" hypothesis in the brief.
- **Novel contribution:** The JMJ24-JMJ25 antagonism reveals that chromatin remodeling at gene bodies is actively balanced. xRNA downregulation of JMJ25 would shift this balance toward JMJ24-mediated silencing at some loci, but the net effect depends on which genes are sensitive to this balance during germination. The connection to RNAi pathways is also noteworthy given that the xRNA itself is an RNA-based agent.
