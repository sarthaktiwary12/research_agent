## Abstract
Cysteine-rich receptor-like kinases (CRKs) belong to a large DUF26-containing receptor-like kinase (RLK) family with more than 40 members in Arabidopsis. CRKs have a tripartite structure: a variable extracellular domain with two DUF26 motifs (C-X8-C-X2-C with conserved Cys residues), a single transmembrane domain, and a conserved intracellular kinase domain. CRKs function in immunity (Ca2+ influx, ROS production, MAPK cascade activation, callose deposition, stomatal immunity, programmed cell death), abiotic stress response, and growth-stress balance. CRK5/CRK22 regulate defense to Verticillium dahliae toxins via the MPK3/6-WRKY70-TGA2/6 pathway. CRK2 forms a complex with NADPH oxidase RBOHD for extracellular ROS production. CRK4/CRK5 enhance drought tolerance via ABA sensitivity. The DUF26 extracellular domain was proposed as a redox sensor, though the activating modification or ligands remain elusive. CRKs represent 44 members in Arabidopsis, 36 in rice, and have been functionally characterized in wheat and tomato. They are central regulators linking ROS signaling, calcium signaling, MAPK cascades, and ABA signaling.
