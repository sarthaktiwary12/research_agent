## Abstract
Growth-defence trade-offs are a major challenge in crop breeding. This review revisits the molecular mechanisms underlying growth-defence trade-offs, focusing on hormone signaling crosstalk (SA/JA vs auxin/BR/GA), NLR-mediated immunity costs, and strategies to decouple the tradeoff in breeding programs. The authors discuss how domestication has altered defence-growth balance in crops, and propose breeding strategies to optimize both growth and resistance. The review covers recent advances in understanding how NLR receptor pair systems can minimize autoimmunity costs, and how temporal/spatial restriction of defence activation reduces growth penalties.
