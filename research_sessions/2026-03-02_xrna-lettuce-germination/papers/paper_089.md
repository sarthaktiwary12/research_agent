# Elucidation of the Role of Nucleolin as a Cell Surface Receptor for Nucleic Acid-Based Adjuvants

## Metadata

- **Year:** 2022
- **Source:** npj Vaccines (Nature)
- **URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41541-022-00541-6
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41541-022-00541-6
- **PMID:** 36202858
- **Citation Count:** N/A
- **Source Database:** Cluster E — WebSearch

## Abstract
Nucleic acid-based adjuvants such as CpG oligonucleotides (CpG ODNs) and poly(I:C) are potential vaccine adjuvants, but the mechanism by which cell surface receptors promote their uptake into dendritic cells and shuttle them to intracellular Toll-like receptors remained to be further investigated. This paper demonstrated a role for nucleolin, a multifunctional DNA- and RNA-binding protein and a major constituent of the nucleolus, as one of the cell-surface receptors for nucleic acid-based adjuvants. Nucleolin on mouse DC surface bound directly to A-type CpG ODN, B-type CpG ODN, and poly(I:C) and promoted their internalization. In human DCs, nucleolin also contributed to binding, internalization, and subsequent cytokine production. Human nucleolin is an abundant multifunctional 76 kDa membrane-associated phosphoprotein with 707 amino acids composed of three main domains: an acidic histone-like NH2 terminus, a central domain containing four RNA-binding sub-domains, and a carboxy terminal domain. Nucleolin-mediated cellular trafficking of DNA nanoparticles is lipid raft and microtubule dependent.

## Deep Reading Notes

### Key Findings
- Nucleolin serves as a cell-surface receptor for nucleic acid-based adjuvants (CpG ODNs and poly(I:C)) on dendritic cells
- Nucleolin on mouse DC surface bound DIRECTLY to A-type CpG ODN, B-type CpG ODN, and poly(I:C), promoting their INTERNALIZATION
- In human DCs, nucleolin contributed to binding, internalization, AND subsequent cytokine production — demonstrating functional uptake
- Nucleolin is an abundant multifunctional 76 kDa membrane-associated phosphoprotein with 707 amino acids
- Three main structural domains: (a) acidic histone-like N-terminus, (b) central domain with FOUR RNA-binding sub-domains (RBDs/RRMs), and (c) carboxy-terminal RGG domain
- Nucleolin-mediated cellular trafficking is LIPID RAFT and MICROTUBULE dependent
- Key context from wider literature: Nucleolin is a HIGH-AFFINITY binder of G-quadruplex structures — its RBD and RGG domains cooperate for G4 recognition; Phe residues in the RGG domain are critical for G4 binding; nucleolin discriminates between different G4 topologies (preferring some over others); nucleolin shows greater affinity for certain G4s than for its canonical NRE RNA binding partner

   - **Section/Page:** Supplementary literature review

### Limitations
- **Stated by authors:** Not available from abstract
- **Additional concerns:** (1) CRITICAL: This study is in ANIMAL immune cells (DCs), not plant cells. Plants have nucleolin homologs but their cell-surface expression and RNA-binding properties have not been demonstrated to be equivalent; (2) The study examines CpG ODNs and poly(I:C) as ligands — these are NOT G-quadruplex-forming RNAs. The connection to G4 binding comes from separate literature; (3) While nucleolin binds G4 structures with high affinity, the paper does not directly test G4 RNA uptake via nucleolin; (4) Plant nucleolin (AtNUC-L1/L2 in Arabidopsis) is primarily nucleolar — cell-surface expression in plants is not established; (5) Lettuce nucleolin homolog characterization is unknown; (6) The endosomal escape rate (~25-30% stated in the brief) is not addressed — how nucleolin-internalized RNAs escape endosomes is a separate question; (7) Lipid raft and microtubule-dependent trafficking may not translate to plant cells which have different membrane organization and cell walls

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** The nucleolin-mediated uptake mechanism for the xRNA drug — this paper demonstrates that nucleolin functions as a cell-surface receptor for nucleic acid internalization, providing the conceptual basis for nucleolin-mediated xRNA uptake
- **Partially addresses:** The specificity of nucleolin for G4 structures (from supplementary literature — nucleolin preferentially binds G4, supporting the idea that G4-forming xRNA tRFs would be preferentially recognized by cell-surface nucleolin)
- **Novel contribution:** Connects three elements of the xRNA drug design: (1) G-quadruplex structure of the tRF, (2) nucleolin as the uptake receptor with G4 binding preference, and (3) endocytic internalization pathway. The lipid raft/microtubule-dependent trafficking provides a potential mechanism for endosomal routing that could be relevant to the ~25-30% endosomal escape rate.
