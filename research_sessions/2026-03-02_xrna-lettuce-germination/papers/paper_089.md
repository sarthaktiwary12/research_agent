# Elucidation of the Role of Nucleolin as a Cell Surface Receptor for Nucleic Acid-Based Adjuvants

## Abstract
Nucleic acid-based adjuvants such as CpG oligonucleotides (CpG ODNs) and poly(I:C) are potential vaccine adjuvants, but the mechanism by which cell surface receptors promote their uptake into dendritic cells and shuttle them to intracellular Toll-like receptors remained to be further investigated. This paper demonstrated a role for nucleolin, a multifunctional DNA- and RNA-binding protein and a major constituent of the nucleolus, as one of the cell-surface receptors for nucleic acid-based adjuvants. Nucleolin on mouse DC surface bound directly to A-type CpG ODN, B-type CpG ODN, and poly(I:C) and promoted their internalization. In human DCs, nucleolin also contributed to binding, internalization, and subsequent cytokine production. Human nucleolin is an abundant multifunctional 76 kDa membrane-associated phosphoprotein with 707 amino acids composed of three main domains: an acidic histone-like NH2 terminus, a central domain containing four RNA-binding sub-domains, and a carboxy terminal domain. Nucleolin-mediated cellular trafficking of DNA nanoparticles is lipid raft and microtubule dependent.
