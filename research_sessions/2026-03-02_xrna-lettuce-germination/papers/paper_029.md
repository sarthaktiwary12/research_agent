## Abstract
PIN-FORMED (PIN) proteins are proposed auxin efflux transporters. PIN-mediated auxin transport is directly activated by D6 PROTEIN KINASE (D6PK) and PINOID (PID)/WAG kinases of the Arabidopsis AGCVIII kinase family. D6PKs and PID have differential phosphosite preferences. PIN activation by protein kinases is a crucial component of auxin transport control. The development and morphology of vascular plants is critically determined by synthesis and proper distribution of the phytohormone auxin, and the directed cell-to-cell distribution is achieved through a system of auxin influx and efflux transporters.

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** Research Question 2 — D6PK function in auxin transport control via PIN phosphorylation. The differential phosphosite preferences between D6PK and PID suggest that D6PK downregulation by xRNA would specifically alter one mode of PIN regulation while PID-dependent phosphorylation remains intact.
- **Partially addresses:** The question of functional redundancy — if D6PK and PID phosphorylate different PIN sites, then D6PK loss cannot be fully compensated by PID, making D6PK a more effective xRNA target.
- **Novel contribution:** The differential phosphosite model is critical for predicting xRNA effects. D6PK downregulation would selectively reduce phosphorylation at D6PK-preferred sites while leaving PID/WAG-dependent phosphorylation intact. This partial phosphorylation state could produce a qualitatively different PIN activity profile, not simply reduced transport. This nuance is important for predicting whether D6PK knockdown would reduce, redirect, or qualitatively alter auxin transport during radicle protrusion.
