## Key Findings

### Clade A PP2C Specificity for ABA Signaling
- PYR/PYL/RCAR ABA receptors SELECTIVELY inhibit Clade A PP2Cs
- Non-Clade A PP2Cs (B, C, D, E, etc.) are NOT targets of ABA receptors
- Six Clade A PP2Cs directly regulated: ABI1, ABI2, HAB1, HAB2, AHG1, PP2CA/AHG3
- Clade A arranged in two subgroups: {ABI1, ABI2, HAB1, HAB2} and {PP2CA/AHG3, AHG1, At5g59220, At1g07430, At2g29380}

### Clade B PP2Cs Are MAPK Phosphatases
- AP2C1 (Clade B) is a negative regulator of stress-induced MAP kinase cascade
- Interacts with and inactivates MPK4 and MPK6
- Clade B PP2Cs showed limited or NO interaction with SnRK2s

### Implications for PP2C47
- If the lettuce PP2C47 is Clade B (as suggested by Medicago MtPP2C47 homology), then:
  - It would NOT be part of the ABA-PYR/PYL-SnRK2 signaling pathway
  - Its downregulation would NOT cause ABA hypersensitivity
  - Instead, it would affect MAPK signaling (wounding, pathogen response)
  - This would RESOLVE the PP2C47 paradox

### The PP2C-SnRK2 Module
- Under normal conditions, Clade A PP2Cs constitutively interact with and inactivate SnRK2s
- ABA receptors (PYR/PYL) bind to PP2Cs upon ABA perception, releasing SnRK2s
- Activated SnRK2s phosphorylate transcription factors (ABI5, ABFs) to activate ABA response genes
- In 14 receptor + 9 PP2C + 3 SnRK2 system: signal gathered by receptors, transduced through PP2Cs and SnRK2s

## Critical Assessment
- This paper provides the strongest evidence that ONLY Clade A PP2Cs are relevant to ABA-mediated germination control
- If PP2C47 is non-Clade A, it is categorically excluded from the ABA signaling pathway
- The "paradox" that PP2C47 downregulation would inhibit germination ONLY applies if it is Clade A
