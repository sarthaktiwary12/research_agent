## Key Findings

### PP2C Gene Family Size and Classification
- Identified 80 PP2C genes in Arabidopsis thaliana and 78 in Oryza sativa
- PP2C is one of the largest protein phosphatase families in plants
- Phylogenetic analysis divided Arabidopsis PP2Cs into 13 subfamilies (A through K/L/M), supported by gene structure and protein motif analyses
- Previously, 76 genes had been divided into 10 subfamilies (A-J); this study added subfamilies K and split F into F1/F2

### Clade-Specific Functions
- **Clade A:** Negative regulators of ABA signaling; inhibit SnRK2 kinases. Includes ABI1, ABI2, HAB1, HAB2, PP2CA/AHG3, AHG1, At5g59220, At1g07430, At2g29380
- **Clade B:** Contains AP2C1-4; MAPK phosphatases involved in wounding and pathogen stress. AP2C1 interacts with MPK4/MPK6. **NOT involved in ABA-SnRK2 signaling**
- **Clade C:** Contains POLTERGEIST (POL) and PLL proteins; regulate stem cell identity
- **Clade D, E, G, H, I, J:** Diverse functions, many not yet characterized
- Subfamilies C and D are sister clades (83% bootstrap support)
- Subfamilies E, L, and H form a monophyletic cluster (86% bootstrap support)

### Relevance to PP2C47 Question
- The PP2C47 designation in Medicago truncatula maps to **Clade B** (not Clade A)
- MtPP2C47 is homologous to Arabidopsis AP2C1, a MAPK phosphatase
- Clade B PP2Cs showed limited or NO interaction with SnRK2s in yeast two-hybrid assays
- **This means if the lettuce PP2C47 is also Clade B, its downregulation would NOT cause the ABA hypersensitivity predicted by Clade A PP2C knockout studies**

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- The PP2C47 paradox (Contradiction 1) could be RESOLVED if the lettuce target is Clade B
- Clade B PP2C downregulation would reduce MAPK pathway suppression, potentially having different (possibly pro-germination) effects unrelated to ABA
- Need to confirm lettuce PP2C47 homology through BLAST analysis against the lettuce genome

## Limitations
- This study covers Arabidopsis and rice only, not lettuce
- PP2C numbering differs across species (sequential by chromosomal position)
- Lettuce-specific PP2C classification has not been published
