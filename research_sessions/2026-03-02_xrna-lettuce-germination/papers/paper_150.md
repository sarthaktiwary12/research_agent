## Key Findings

### Ribosome Composition
- 80S ribosomes = 40S (small) + 60S (large) subunit
- 40S: 18S rRNA + ribosomal proteins (RPSs)
- 60S: 5.8S + 25S/28S rRNAs + 5S rRNA + ribosomal proteins (RPLs)
- ~80 ribosomal proteins total

### rRNA Production by Pol I
- 45S rDNA transcription by Pol I produces precursor for 18S, 5.8S, and 25S rRNAs
- 5S rRNA transcribed separately by Pol III
- Pol I transcription requires numerous general transcription factors including TBP

### Ribosome Assembly Pathway
- Hundreds of specific assembly factors required for 40S and 60S subunit maturation
- Assembly occurs in nucleolus, nucleoplasm, and cytoplasm
- Co-transcriptional rRNA folding accelerates Pol I elongation
- Transport from nucleolus to cytoplasm involves multiple export factors

### Ribosome Biogenesis During Development
- Ribosome production is one of the most energy-intensive cellular processes
- Actively dividing cells require massive ribosome production
- During seed germination: transition from stored ribosomes to newly synthesized ones
- Nucleolar size and activity directly reflect ribosome biogenesis rate

### Connection to Nucleolin/NUC1
- Nucleolin (NUC1) is the most abundant nucleolar protein
- Required for rRNA processing and ribosome assembly
- nuc1 mutants show nucleolar disorganization and altered pre-rRNA accumulation
- Both NUC1 and NUC2 are essential (double mutant lethal)

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- Pol I subunit downregulation would reduce rRNA transcription rate
- Combined with NUC1-related disruption (if RGG target = nucleolin), ribosome biogenesis could be significantly impaired
- During germination, seeds initially rely on stored ribosomes (from maturation)
- The "second wave" of protein synthesis (after 12h) requires new ribosome production
- xRNA-mediated reduction of Pol I subunit + nucleolin could create a ribosome biogenesis bottleneck
- This would SLOW germination (anti-xRNA) unless the targets are carefully selected to avoid this outcome
- **Risk assessment**: If Pol I subunit is a shared subunit (with Pol II/III/IV/V), effects could be catastrophic

## Limitations
- No direct study of ribosome biogenesis rate during seed germination time course
- The specific Pol I subunit targeted by xRNA is not identified
- Whether stored ribosomes are sufficient for normal germination completion is unknown
