# BTB/POZ domain protein GmBTB/POZ promotes the ubiquitination and degradation of the soybean AP2/ERF-like transcription factor GmAP2 to regulate the defense response to Phytophthora sojae

## Abstract
BTB/POZ (Broad-complex, Tramtrack and Bric a brac / Pox virus and Zinc finger) domain proteins are evolutionarily conserved scaffolding proteins that can act as E3-ubiquitin intermediates, binding substrate proteins to bridge them to CULLIN 3-RING E3 ligase complexes for ubiquitination and proteasomal degradation. In Arabidopsis, BTB/POZ-MATH (BPM) proteins form a six-member family that assembles with CUL3a/CUL3b as substrate adaptors and interacts with ERF/AP2 transcription factors. GmBTB/POZ in soybean enhances resistance to Phytophthora sojae via salicylic acid (SA), promoting ubiquitination and degradation of both GmAP2 transcription factor and GmLHP1 (LIKE HETEROCHROMATIN PROTEIN1). BPMs also mediate DREB2A proteolysis to modulate thermotolerance. The BTB/POZ domain of NPR1 interacts with the TGA2 repression domain in disease resistance. POB1 (BTB-BACK domain protein) suppresses immune cell death by targeting ubiquitin E3 ligase PUB17 for degradation. This demonstrates synergy between BTB/POZ-mediated ubiquitination and AP2/ERF-mediated defense pathways.

### Defense Signaling Adaptor Role (Key for Research Brief)
- **BTB/POZ as defense signaling hub:** BTB/POZ proteins sit at the interface of protein degradation and defense transcription factor regulation:
  - SA pathway: NPR1 (BTB domain) → TGA2 → PR gene expression
  - SA pathway: NPR3/NPR4 (BTB-CUL3 adaptors) → NPR1 degradation (negative regulation)
  - JA pathway: POB1/POB2 → PUB17 degradation → suppression of immune cell death
  - ERF/AP2 pathway: BPMs → ERF/AP2 degradation → modulation of defense gene expression
  - Thermotolerance: BPMs → DREB2A degradation
- **For xRNA downregulation of BTB/POZ:** Reducing BTB/POZ protein levels would:
  - Impair CUL3-BTB E3 ligase substrate recognition → stabilize defense-related transcription factors (ERF/AP2, DREB2A)
  - If the target BTB is a negative defense regulator (like POB1): downregulation → enhanced defense → ANTI-growth
  - If the target BTB is a positive defense regulator (like GmBTB/POZ): downregulation → reduced defense → PRO-growth
  - If the target BTB is like BPMs targeting ERF/AP2: downregulation → ERF/AP2 stabilization → enhanced defense transcription → ANTI-growth
  - **Direction depends entirely on which BTB protein is the xRNA target and which substrate it degrades**

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** The "BTB/POZ: Defense signaling adaptor role" focus area — this paper establishes BTB/POZ proteins as substrate adaptors for CUL3-RING E3 ligases that regulate defense transcription factors
- **Partially addresses:** Research question 3 (Defense Reallocation) — BTB/POZ proteins modulate the balance between defense and growth through targeted degradation of signaling components
- **Novel contribution:** The key insight is that BTB/POZ proteins operate at a REGULATORY level (controlling turnover of defense transcription factors) rather than a METABOLIC level (unlike TPS or FMO enzymes that consume metabolic precursors). Downregulating a BTB protein would affect the stability of its target proteins, creating cascading effects on defense gene expression. This is qualitatively different from the metabolic reallocation mechanism of TPS/FMO downregulation.
