# Regulation of Membrane Trafficking, Cytoskeleton Dynamics, and Cell Polarity by ROP/RAC GTPases

## Abstract
Activated GTP-bound ROP/RAC GTPases accumulate at the plasma membrane at the apex of expanding cells based on lipid modification of the hypervariable domain and interactions with the membrane lipid PtdIns 4,5-P2. The ROP/RAC-interacting scaffold protein ICR1 binds to the exocyst subunit SEC3, directly linking ROP activation to the control of secretion. Elevated cytoplasmic Ca2+ levels depolymerize F-actin structures and promote the fusion of secretory vesicles with the plasma membrane. RAC/ROP GTPases serve as central molecular switches coordinating actin cytoskeleton dynamics, vesicle trafficking (including exocytosis via the exocyst complex), and polar growth in tip-growing cells such as pollen tubes and root hairs.
