## Abstract
Some pathogens and pests deliver small RNAs (sRNAs) into host cells to suppress host immunity. Conversely, hosts also transfer sRNAs into pathogens and pests to inhibit their virulence. The authors showed that Arabidopsis shuttles defensive sRNAs into the necrotrophic fungus Botrytis cinerea via extracellular vesicles (EVs). Transferred host sRNAs contribute to host immunity by silencing fungal genes. Plant extracellular vesicles, especially exosomes, play an essential role in cross-kingdom sRNA trafficking between Arabidopsis and the fungal pathogen. TET8- and TET9-associated exosomes contribute to plant immunity against fungal infection by transferring host sRNAs.
