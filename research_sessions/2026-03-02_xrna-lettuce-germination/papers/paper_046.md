## Abstract
The researchers systematically analyzed nucleotide compositions of transcriptomes across 1,000 plants (1KP project) and found that plants growing in cold climates have guanine (G)-enriched transcriptomes, which are prone to forming RNA G-quadruplex structures. Both immunofluorescence detection and in vivo structure profiling revealed that RNA G-quadruplex formation in plants was globally enhanced in response to cold. Cold-responsive RNA G-quadruplexes strongly enhanced mRNA stability rather than affecting translation. Disruption of individual RNA G-quadruplex structures promoted mRNA decay in the cold, leading to impaired plant cold response.

## Key Findings
- Plants in cold climates have G-enriched transcriptomes prone to RNA G-quadruplex (rG4) formation
- rG4 formation is globally enhanced in response to cold across plant species
- Cold-responsive rG4s enhance mRNA stability (not translation regulation)
- Disruption of individual rG4 structures promotes mRNA decay and impairs cold response
- Systematic analysis across 1,000 plant species (1KP project)

## Relevance to xRNA Research
Relevant to understanding how the G-quadruplex structure of the xRNA drug contributes to its stability in the plant cellular environment. The finding that rG4s enhance mRNA stability suggests that the xRNA drug's G4 structure may protect it from degradation inside plant cells, prolonging its gene-silencing activity. Also relevant to the broader question of how rG4 structures interact with plant RNA stability pathways — the xRNA drug could potentially compete with or mimic endogenous rG4-mediated RNA stabilization mechanisms.
