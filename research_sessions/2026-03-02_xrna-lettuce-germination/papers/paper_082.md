## Abstract
Noncoding RNAs can travel through not only plasmodesma and phloem but also intercellular barriers to regulate distinct processes. Increasing evidence shows that the intercellular trans-kingdom transmission of ncRNAs is able to modulate many important interactions between plants and other organisms, such as plant response to pathogen attack, the symbiosis between legume plants and rhizobia, and the interactions with parasitic plants. In these interactions, plant ncRNAs are believed to be sorted into extracellular vesicles (EVs) or other nonvesicular vehicles to pass through cell barriers and trigger trans-kingdom RNA interference (RNAi) in recipient cells from different species. Plant-derived sRNAs can be transported to fungi as naked sRNAs, or bound to RNA-binding proteins (RBP), or enclosed inside extracellular vesicles. The review discusses the current research on the cross-species regulatory effects of plant miRNAs on nearby plants, pathogenic fungi, and insects.

- **Additional concerns:** (1) Review article — no new primary data; (2) Trans-kingdom RNAi remains partially controversial, particularly regarding: quantitative efficiency of RNA transfer, whether observed effects are physiologically significant vs. experimental artifacts, and specificity of transferred RNAs; (3) The review covers plant-to-fungus, plant-to-bacterium, plant-to-plant, and plant-to-insect — but does NOT address exogenous-drug-to-plant transfer (the reverse direction relevant to xRNA); (4) EV-mediated transport is emphasized but the efficiency of non-vesicular transport is not well quantified; (5) No discussion of G-quadruplex structures in transported RNAs; (6) No discussion of nucleolin or receptor-mediated uptake

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** The general concept of trans-kingdom ncRNA communication — provides context that small RNAs can move between kingdoms and trigger gene silencing in recipients
- **Partially addresses:** Transport mechanisms for extracellular RNAs (EVs, RBPs, naked RNA); the RNAi machinery in recipient cells that processes foreign small RNAs
- **Novel contribution:** Consolidates the evidence base for multiple parallel RNA transport pathways (EV, RBP-bound, naked), which is relevant to understanding how the xRNA drug (applied as a free G4-forming tRF) could reach plant cell interiors. The existence of multiple uptake pathways suggests the xRNA drug need not rely on a single receptor mechanism.
