## Key Findings

### CSLG/CslM Are Glucuronosyltransferases, NOT Cell Wall Enzymes
- Cellulose synthase-like G (CslG) / CslM proteins are glucuronosyltransferases
- They catalyze **3-O-glucuronosylation of triterpenoid aglycones** (saponin biosynthesis)
- This represents "co-option" of cell wall biosynthesis machinery for specialized metabolism
- Plants "hijacked" an enzyme from the cellulose synthesis machinery

### Enzyme Function
- CSyGT (cellulose synthase superfamily-derived glycosyltransferase) is the functional designation
- Substrate: triterpenoid aglycones (oleanane-type in legumes)
- Product: glucuronosylated saponins
- The reaction involves UDP-glucuronic acid as sugar donor
- ER-membrane localized (unlike cytosolic GT glycosyltransferases)

### CslG vs. CslM Classification
- CslG/CslM nomenclature is debated — CslM is a newly defined subfamily
- Some researchers classify these as CslG, others as CslM
- CslM1 and CslM2 have metabolically distinct functions
- CslG sensu stricto may be restricted to non-grass species

### Taxonomic Distribution
- CslB and CslG are specific to non-grass species (dicots)
- CslF, CslH, CslJ found only in grasses
- CslA, CslC, CslD are common to all land plants
- **No CslM genes in Arabidopsis thaliana** (important: means Arabidopsis data not directly transferable)
- Spinach CslG (SOAP5) characterized for triterpenoid saponin biosynthesis

### Saponin Production and Plant Biology
- Saponins are natural products with triterpene core + glycosylations
- Bioactivities: anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antifungal, immunostimulatory
- In licorice: CSyGT required for glycyrrhizin (low-calorie sweetener) production
- In soybean: CSyGT required for soyasaponin production
- CSyGT mutants of Lotus japonicus do not accumulate soyasaponin

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- **The CSLG3 target is NOT a cell wall structural enzyme** — it is a saponin biosynthesis enzyme
- This fundamentally reclassifies the target from "cell wall & seed coat" to "specialized metabolism / defense"
- Downregulation of CSLG3 in lettuce would reduce triterpenoid saponin production
- Lettuce (Asteraceae) produces sesquiterpene lactones — CSLG3 may be involved in their glycosylation
- If CSLG3 is involved in defense compound biosynthesis, its downregulation fits the "defense reallocation" theme
- This moves CSLG3 from Research Question 6 (Cell Wall) to Research Question 3 (Defense Reallocation)

## Limitations
- The specific CSLG3 in lettuce has not been characterized
- Whether lettuce CSLG homologs function as glucuronosyltransferases or retain cell wall activity is unknown
- Asteraceae sesquiterpene lactones may use different glycosylation machinery than legume saponins
