## Abstract
This review comprehensively covers the biogenesis and biological functions of plant tRNA-derived fragments (tRFs). In plants, tRFs are generated through two main pathways: DCL1-dependent (confirmed by reduction of tRFAla-5a in dcl1-11 mutant pollen) and RNS1-dependent (Dicer-independent, induced by ABA). The review highlights that several tRFs mediate degradation of transcripts through the AGO1 pathway based on sequence complementarity between tRFs and their target regions. Target transcripts include protein-coding transcripts and retrotransposon transcripts. In pollen cells, 19-nt tRF-5s bind AGO1 to mediate silencing of transposable element (TE) transcripts in a DCL1-dependent manner. Knockdown of 5'tRF-MetCAT with STTM (short tandem target mimic) inhibits target RNA cleavage, providing strong evidence for AGO-dependent target regulation. Additionally, 19-nt 5'tRF-AlaAGC suppresses target gene expression through AGO1, validated by Northern blot and 5' RACE. The review also discusses cross-kingdom gene silencing, where rhizobial tRFs transfer to soybean roots and hijack host RNAi machinery to silence key host genes, enhancing nodulation.
