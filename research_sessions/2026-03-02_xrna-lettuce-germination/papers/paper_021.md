# Three Arabidopsis SnRK2 Protein Kinases, SRK2D/SnRK2.2, SRK2E/SnRK2.6/OST1 and SRK2I/SnRK2.3, Involved in ABA Signaling Are Essential for the Control of Seed Development and Dormancy

## Abstract
ABA is an important phytohormone regulating stress tolerance, seed development and germination. SRK2D/SnRK2.2, SRK2E/SnRK2.6/OST1 and SRK2I/SnRK2.3 are redundant ABA-activated SnRK2s in Arabidopsis. These SnRK2 proteins were mainly expressed in the nucleus during seed development and germination. The triple mutant (srk2d srk2e srk2i) was sensitive to desiccation and showed severe growth defects during seed development, exhibiting a loss of dormancy and elevated seed ABA content. This demonstrates the essential role of SnRK2 kinases in seed development, dormancy, and germination control.

### CRITICAL ANALYSIS: The SnRK2-Dormancy Link and Its Implications for PP2C47

**This paper provides the RECIPROCAL evidence to the PP2C papers (004, 005, 020):**

The logic chain is now complete:
- PP2C active → SnRK2 inactive → NO dormancy → germination proceeds (papers 004, 005, 020)
- PP2C absent/inhibited → SnRK2 active → dormancy MAINTAINED → germination delayed (papers 004, 005)
- SnRK2 absent (triple mutant) → NO dormancy → seeds germinate precociously (THIS paper)

**The triple mutant phenotype is the definitive proof:** When SnRK2 kinases are removed, seeds LOSE dormancy — they cannot maintain the dormant state even when ABA is elevated. This confirms that SnRK2 activity is REQUIRED for dormancy, and therefore anything that ACTIVATES SnRK2 (including PP2C downregulation) would PROMOTE dormancy.

**Apparent paradox and resolution:**
- The brief says PP2C47 downregulation should ACCELERATE germination
- But PP2C downregulation → SnRK2 activation → ENHANCED dormancy
- The SnRK2 triple mutant (SnRK2 loss) → LOSS of dormancy → faster/precocious germination

**This means:** To accelerate germination via this pathway, one would want to DOWNREGULATE SnRK2 (or UPREGULATE PP2C), not the reverse.

**Key nuance — elevated ABA in SnRK2 triple mutant:** The triple mutant has ELEVATED ABA levels despite loss of dormancy. This is because ABA signaling is interrupted downstream — the seeds produce more ABA as a feedback response but cannot respond to it. This shows that ABA level alone is insufficient for dormancy — the signaling machinery (SnRK2) must be intact. If PP2C47 is downregulated, SnRK2 becomes hyperactive, and even NORMAL levels of ABA would trigger strong dormancy responses.
