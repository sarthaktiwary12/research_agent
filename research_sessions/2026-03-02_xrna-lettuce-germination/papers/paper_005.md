## Abstract
This study identifies AHG3/AtPP2CA as the most active PP2C gene in Arabidopsis seeds and demonstrates that its disruption causes the strongest ABA hypersensitivity during germination among all PP2C knockout mutants tested. Nine protein phosphatases are classified as clade A PP2Cs in Arabidopsis, and six (ABI1, ABI2, AHG1, AHG3/PP2CA, HAB1, HAB2) are involved in ABA signaling. All knockout mutants exhibited significant ABA hypersensitivity, confirming they are negative regulators of ABA signaling. AHG3 is constitutively expressed during seed development and germination, while AHG1 is gradually activated during seed development. AHG3 is expressed more strongly in specific tissues such as meristems and vascular bundles, while AHG1 is expressed uniformly in the embryo. The disruptant mutant of AHG3/AtPP2CA showed the strongest ABA hypersensitivity during germination but did not display significant phenotypes in adult plants, indicating a seed-specific role. This finding is directly relevant to understanding how PP2C silencing would affect germination behavior.

### CRITICAL ANALYSIS: Direct Relevance to PP2C47 and xRNA Targeting

**This paper provides the most definitive systematic evidence on PP2C function during germination:**

Key conclusion: **EVERY clade A PP2C knockout causes ABA hypersensitivity during germination.** This means:
- LOF of any clade A PP2C → seeds more sensitive to ABA → germination INHIBITED/DELAYED
- The more potent the PP2C (like AHG3), the stronger the ABA hypersensitivity upon knockout

**For xRNA PP2C47 targeting:** If PP2C47 is a clade A PP2C (as the brief's functional description — "SnRK2 dephosphorylation" — indicates), then its downregulation by xRNA would make lettuce seeds MORE sensitive to ABA, which would INHIBIT germination, especially under thermoinhibition conditions where ABA is already elevated.

**However, there is a nuance for lettuce specifically:** Under NORMAL (non-stress) germination conditions where endogenous ABA is low, the effect of PP2C downregulation may be minimal because SnRK2 activation requires ABA-triggered signaling. The PP2C-SnRK2 module may be most relevant under stress (heat/thermoinhibition). Under favorable conditions with low ABA, SnRK2s may not be significantly activated even with reduced PP2C, because the PYR/PYL receptors also need ABA to bind PP2Cs.

**The seed-specific phenotype of AHG3** is relevant: it shows that germination-active PP2Cs can be functionally important only during seed stages. If PP2C47 in lettuce is similarly seed-specific, its downregulation specifically during imbibition could have disproportionate effects.

### Limitations
- **Stated by authors:** Not accessible from abstract
- **Additional concerns:** Study in Arabidopsis — lettuce PP2C family may have different members with different tissue specificities. The 9-member clade A in Arabidopsis may not correspond directly to lettuce PP2C complement. Lettuce genome is much larger (~2.5 Gb vs. ~135 Mb in Arabidopsis) and may have expanded PP2C families with neofunctionalized or subfunctionalized members.
