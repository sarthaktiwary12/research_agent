## Abstract
This study identifies AHG3/AtPP2CA as the most active PP2C gene in Arabidopsis seeds and demonstrates that its disruption causes the strongest ABA hypersensitivity during germination among all PP2C knockout mutants tested. Nine protein phosphatases are classified as clade A PP2Cs in Arabidopsis, and six (ABI1, ABI2, AHG1, AHG3/PP2CA, HAB1, HAB2) are involved in ABA signaling. All knockout mutants exhibited significant ABA hypersensitivity, confirming they are negative regulators of ABA signaling. AHG3 is constitutively expressed during seed development and germination, while AHG1 is gradually activated during seed development. AHG3 is expressed more strongly in specific tissues such as meristems and vascular bundles, while AHG1 is expressed uniformly in the embryo. The disruptant mutant of AHG3/AtPP2CA showed the strongest ABA hypersensitivity during germination but did not display significant phenotypes in adult plants, indicating a seed-specific role. This finding is directly relevant to understanding how PP2C silencing would affect germination behavior.
