# The Polarly Localized D6 PROTEIN KINASE Is Required for Efficient Auxin Transport in Arabidopsis thaliana

## Abstract
Directional auxin transport and auxin responses are required for proper embryogenesis, organ formation, vascular development, and tropisms. A family of four highly homologous AGC protein kinases was designated the D6 protein kinases (D6PKs). D6PK localizes to the basal (lower) membrane of Arabidopsis root cells, where it colocalizes with PIN1, PIN2, and PIN4. D6PK and PIN1 interact genetically, and D6PK phosphorylates PIN proteins in vitro and in vivo. The data show that D6PK is required for efficient auxin transport and suggest that PIN proteins are D6PK phosphorylation targets.
