## Abstract
The mechanisms by which lettuce controls seed germination under water stress had remained unknown. Germination percentage was evaluated in a Lactuca sativa (cv. Salinas) x Lactuca serriola (US96UC23) recombinant inbred line (RIL) population using 10% polyethylene glycol (PEG). Two quantitative trait loci (QTL) for germination percentage were identified on chromosomes 4 and 8 in the RIL population. Transcriptome analysis of US96UC23 (the stress-tolerant wild parent) facilitated the discovery of significant GO terms, KEGG pathways, and gene networks associated with seed germination under osmotic stress in lettuce. Sequencing was performed on the Illumina NovaSeq 6000 platform; reads were mapped to the lettuce reference genome (V. 8.0) using HISAT2.
