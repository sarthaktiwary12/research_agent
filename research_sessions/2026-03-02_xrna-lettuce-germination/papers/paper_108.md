## Abstract
Translation of seed stored mRNAs is essential to trigger germination. However, when RNAPII re-engages RNA synthesis during the seed-to-seedling transition had remained in question. By combining csRNA-seq, ATAC-seq, and smFISH in Arabidopsis thaliana, the authors demonstrated that active transcription initiation is detectable during the entire germination process. Features of non-coding regulation such as dynamic changes in chromatin accessible regions, antisense transcription, and bidirectional non-coding promoters are widespread throughout the Arabidopsis genome. Sensitivity to exogenous ABA during germination depends on proximal promoter accessibility at ABA-responsive genes. The results reveal that active enhancer elements are transcribed producing non-coding enhancer RNAs (eRNAs), as widely documented in metazoans. Transcription re-starts much earlier than previously anticipated after seed re-hydration.
