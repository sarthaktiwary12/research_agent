## Key Findings

### Epigenetic Reprogramming During Germination
- DNA methylation globally high during seed dormancy (MET1, CMT3, RdDM pathways active)
- CHH methylation lower in endosperm than embryo
- Proposed: TE hypomethylation in endosperm generates mobile sRNAs that reinforce silencing in embryo
- When imbibition starts: maturation/dormancy genes silenced by H3K27me3
- Transcriptional landscape modified to stimulate germination and seedling growth

### Small RNA Dynamics
- siRNA stability during germination: ~46% were 21 nt, ~54% were 22 nt
- siRNAs remained relatively stable even in low-viability seeds
- siRNA targets involved in: cytochrome complex function, root development, cell maturation, carbohydrate metabolism
- Despite RNA degradation in aged seeds, siRNAs maintained — potential role in dormancy release

### VIGS via Seed Imbibition
- Virus-induced gene silencing (VIGS) established via seed imbibition (Si-VIGS)
- TRV (tobacco rattle virus) used to silence genes during cotton germination
- Demonstrates that gene silencing machinery is active during seed imbibition
- **Key for xRNA**: Proves that exogenous nucleic acids CAN trigger silencing during germination

### Stored mRNAs and Translation Control
- De novo protein synthesis is required for germination (cycloheximide blocks germination)
- Stored mRNAs from seed maturation provide templates for early protein synthesis
- No correlation between transcript and protein levels during germination (r^2 = 0.02)
- This means translational and post-translational regulation dominates early germination

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- The epigenetic and small RNA regulatory landscape is actively being reconfigured during imbibition
- Gene silencing machinery (DCL, AGO, RDR) is functional during seed germination
- Si-VIGS proof-of-concept: exogenous RNA can enter seeds during imbibition and trigger silencing
- Stored mRNAs are the primary translation templates early in germination — xRNA tRFs could target these stored mRNAs
- The low transcript-protein correlation during germination means that mRNA-level silencing has complex effects on protein output
