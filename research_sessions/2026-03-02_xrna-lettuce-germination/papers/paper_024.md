## Abstract
Almost all mRNA m6A modification is catalyzed by a multiprotein complex containing MTA (ortholog of human METTL3), MTB (METTL14), FIP37 (WTAP), VIRILIZER, HAKAI, and HIZ2, and removed by the m6A demethylases ALKBH10B and ALKBH9B. Deletion of MTA, MTB, FIP37, or VIR stops embryogenesis at the globular stage. HAKAI was identified as a conserved E3 ubiquitin ligase component of the m6A writer complex. The absence of HAKAI decreases the abundance of m6A modifications but does not result in obvious growth defects. The reduced expression and knockout of m6A writers produce distinct differentiation phenotypes, including increase in trichome branches, defective leaf initiation, over-proliferation of the vegetative shoot apical meristem, and embryonic lethality.
