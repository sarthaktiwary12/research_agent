## Key Findings

### Complete PP2C Clade Functional Map
- **Clade A**: ABA signaling negative regulators; bind PYR/PYL receptors; dephosphorylate SnRK2s
  - Members: ABI1, ABI2, HAB1, HAB2, AHG1, AHG3/PP2CA, HAI1, HAI2, HAI3
  - AHG1 is atypical: refractory to RCAR receptor inhibition
  - Subfamilies A ONLY exist in plants
- **Clade B**: MAPK phosphatases; negatively regulate innate immunity
  - Members: AP2C1, AP2C2, AP2C3, AP2C4 (and possibly others)
  - AP2C1 inactivates MPK4 and MPK6
  - Subfamily B ONLY exists in plants
  - Involved in wounding response, pathogen defense, cell division
- **Clade C**: POLTERGEIST/PLL; stem cell maintenance
  - POL and PLL1-5 regulate CLV signaling in shoot apical meristem
- **Clade D**: Includes KAPP (kinase-associated protein phosphatase)
  - Interacts with receptor-like kinases (RLKs)
  - Role in brassinosteroid signaling
- **Clade E**: Largely uncharacterized; some members involved in drought response
- **Clade F**: Split into F1 and F2; diverse functions
- **Clade G-K**: Largely uncharacterized in most species

### Key Distinction: Clade A vs. All Others
- ABA receptor PYR/PYL/RCAR proteins SELECTIVELY inhibit Clade A PP2Cs
- Non-Clade A PP2Cs are NOT part of ABA core signaling
- This selectivity means that the PP2C47 clade assignment is DECISIVE for predicting germination effects

### PP2C in Plant-Specific Contexts
- Subfamilies A and B are plant-specific (no animal/fungal homologs)
- PP2Cs are Mg2+/Mn2+-dependent Ser/Thr phosphatases
- Distinct from PPP family phosphatases (PP1, PP2A, PP2B)
- No sequence homology with PPP family despite similar catalytic chemistry

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- Provides definitive functional framework for PP2C clade classification
- **If PP2C47 = Clade A**: Downregulation = ABA hypersensitivity = DELAYED germination (anti-xRNA)
- **If PP2C47 = Clade B**: Downregulation = reduced MAPK dephosphorylation = enhanced immune signaling / altered defense (compatible with defense-reallocation theme)
- **If PP2C47 = Clade C**: Downregulation = altered stem cell maintenance (unlikely to affect germination directly)
- **If PP2C47 = Clade D**: Downregulation = altered RLK signaling (complex, unpredictable effects)
- The evidence from Medicago (paper_133) placing PP2C47 in Clade B makes the defense/MAPK pathway the most likely scenario

## Limitations
- 2013 review; some newer clade members may have been characterized since
- Lettuce-specific PP2C clade assignments not included
- Functional characterization of clades E-K remains incomplete
