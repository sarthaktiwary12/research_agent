## Abstract
Nucleus-encoded chloroplast preproteins are imported via sequential interactions between their N-terminal transit peptides and the translocons at the outer (TOC) and inner (TIC) envelope membranes. This general import pathway mediates import of approximately 3,500 polypeptides. The TOC complex (Toc75/Toc34/Toc159 at 4:4:1 or 3:3:1 ratio) handles preprotein recognition via GTPase receptors. The TIC complex composition has been debated, with Tic20, Tic21, and/or Tic110 proposed as major pore-forming subunits. A 1 MDa TIC complex contains the chloroplast-encoded Tic214/Ycf1 (notably absent in Poaceae/grasses). Tic236 provides a physical link between TOC and TIC via anchorage in the inner envelope membrane. The TOC/TIC pore exceeds 25.6 angstroms, large enough to import folded proteins, unlike mitochondrial and bacterial translocons. Import is regulated throughout development and by the ubiquitin-proteasome system via CHLORAD (chloroplast-associated protein degradation). Grasses lack Tic214, Ycf2, Tic100, and Tic56, implying alternative translocon complexes exist. This structural study resolves the architecture of the TOC-TIC translocon supercomplex at molecular detail.

### Limitations
- **Stated by authors:** Not assessable from abstract
- **Additional concerns:** (1) TIC32 — our specific target of interest — is NOT mentioned in the abstract. TIC32 (Tic32) is a short-chain dehydrogenase/reductase associated with the TIC complex that is thought to regulate import in a redox- and NADPH-dependent manner. Its absence from the cryo-EM structure may mean it is either a peripheral/regulatory subunit not captured in the purification or it has been reclassified. (2) The cryo-EM structure captures a static snapshot — dynamic regulation of import during developmental transitions (like germination) is not addressed. (3) Lettuce is an asterid (not a grass), so it likely has the full Tic214/Ycf1-containing complex, but lettuce-specific structural features are unknown. (4) The study does not address how import capacity changes during the seed-to-seedling transition.

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** Research question 5 — TIC32/TOC-TIC chloroplast protein import regulation. Although TIC32 is not mentioned in the abstract, this paper provides the structural framework for understanding the import machinery that TIC32 regulates.
- **Partially addresses:** The broader "organelle & energy tuning" module. Since ~3,500 proteins transit through TOC-TIC, modulating this gateway would have sweeping effects on chloroplast biogenesis.
- **Novel contribution:** The critical insight is the SCALE of chloroplast import dependency — ~3,500 proteins depend on TOC-TIC. This means even modest downregulation of a regulatory subunit like TIC32 could create a significant bottleneck in chloroplast protein accumulation during the rapid biogenesis phase of germination/greening. The CHLORAD regulatory mechanism (ubiquitin-proteasome control of TOC receptors) shows that import IS developmentally regulated — xRNA-mediated TIC32 downregulation would be adding another layer of regulation to an already tunable system. Furthermore, since TIC32 specifically responds to NADPH/redox status, its downregulation could decouple import from redox state, forcing a more conservative (slower) import rate regardless of cellular redox conditions.
