## Key Findings

### Study Design
- Germination under PEG-induced water stress in cv. Salinas (L. sativa) x US96UC23 (L. serriola) RIL population
- Two QTLs for germination percentage on chromosomes 4 and 8
- RNA-Seq of wild lettuce (US96UC23) under control (water) and treatment (10% PEG) conditions
- 4,095 differentially expressed genes (DEGs) identified

### Key Candidate Genes Relevant to xRNA Targets
- **SnRK2.3 (AT5G66880.1 homolog)**: Identified as a candidate gene for germination under water stress. SnRK2.3 is ABA-induced and involved in ABA signaling — directly connected to the PP2C-SnRK2 module
- **SnRK1-interacting proteins**: AT5G11460.1 interacted with SnRK1, a central plant growth regulator
- These connect directly to the ABA signaling pathway that is central to the xRNA mechanism

### Germination Response
- Mean germination percentage difference of 35.3% between control and PEG treatment (P = 1.85e-06)
- Transcriptomic response includes ABA signaling, stress response, and metabolic pathways

### Data Availability
- Raw RNA-Seq data: European Nucleotide Archive accession PRJEB71667
- This dataset could potentially be mined for expression levels of ALL xRNA target genes in lettuce during germination

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- **First lettuce-specific RNA-Seq dataset relevant to seed germination transcriptomics**
- 4,095 DEGs provide a landscape of genes active during lettuce germination
- SnRK2.3 identification confirms that the ABA-SnRK2 pathway is active during lettuce germination
- The raw data (PRJEB71667) could be re-analyzed to check expression of specific xRNA targets
- Water stress germination shares hormonal regulation with thermoinhibition (both ABA-mediated)
- However, this is stress-specific transcriptome, not normal germination time course

## Limitations
- Wild lettuce (L. serriola) transcriptome, not cultivated lettuce (L. sativa)
- Stress treatment (PEG), not normal germination conditions
- Only two conditions compared (control vs. PEG); no time-course during germination
- Abstract-level information only; full DEG list not accessible
