## Key Findings

### AtRGGA Function
- AtRGGA regulates tolerance to salt and drought stress in Arabidopsis
- Ubiquitously expressed (seedlings, roots, leaves, inflorescences, siliques)
- YFP-AtRGGA fusion protein localized to cytoplasm of root apex cells

### Tissue Distribution
- Promoter-GUS analysis showed expression in:
  - Root tips (high expression)
  - Leaf vasculature
  - Inflorescences
  - Siliques
  - Seedlings (throughout)
- This ubiquitous expression suggests roles in general stress tolerance rather than tissue-specific development

### Stress Response
- AtRGGA mutants show altered salt and drought tolerance
- RGG domain proteins in plants interact with diverse RNA substrates
- May regulate stress-responsive mRNA stability or translation

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- If the xRNA RGG target is an AtRGGA homolog in lettuce:
  - It would be expressed in root tips (including radicle) — relevant for germination
  - Downregulation could affect stress tolerance during germination
  - May alter translation of stress-responsive mRNAs during imbibition
- The cytoplasmic localization distinguishes it from nucleolar RGG proteins (like nucleolin)
- Stress tolerance during germination is relevant to the xRNA project's goal of enhancing germination vigor

## Limitations
- No data on AtRGGA function during seed germination specifically
- Stress tolerance role established in mature plants, not seeds
- The specific RGG target in the xRNA project is not definitively identified
