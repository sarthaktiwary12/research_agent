## Abstract
Abscisic acid (ABA) and the protein DELAY OF GERMINATION 1 (DOG1) are essential regulators of dormancy. The function of ABA in dormancy is rather well understood, but the role of DOG1 is still unknown. This study describes four phosphatases that interact with DOG1 in seeds. Two of them belong to clade A of type 2C protein phosphatases (PP2Cs): ABA-HYPERSENSITIVE GERMINATION 1 (AHG1) and AHG3. These phosphatases have redundant but essential roles in the release of seed dormancy epistatic to DOG1. Genetic analysis indicates that AHG1 and AHG3 act downstream of DOG1 and are essential for its function. The ABA and DOG1 dormancy pathways converge at clade A of type 2C protein phosphatases. The core ABA signaling pathway consists of RCAR receptors that bind ABA and consequently inhibit PP2C phosphatases, preventing inhibitory dephosphorylation of SnRK2 kinases. AHG1 is unique because it is refractory to RCAR inhibition but negatively regulates ABA responses in vivo.
