## Abstract
Seed dormancy has played a significant role in adaptation and evolution of seed plants. While its biological significance is clear, molecular mechanisms underlying seed dormancy induction, maintenance and alleviation still remain elusive. Intensive efforts have been made to investigate gibberellin and abscisic acid metabolism in seeds. Other mechanisms, possibly independent of hormones or specific to the seed dormancy pathway, are also emerging from genetic analysis. Chromatin remodeling through histone ubiquitination, methylation, and acetylation may play a significant role in seed dormancy regulation. Chemical induction of NCED was sufficient to suppress precocious germination. A positive feedback mechanism for NCED expression in seeds amplifies ABA biosynthesis and signaling, causing hyperdormancy. The review predicts epigenetic regulation mechanisms including long non-coding RNA regulation of DOG1.
