## Key Findings

### PP2C47 in Medicago Is Clade B
- 94 MtPP2C genes identified in Medicago truncatula, classified into 13 subfamilies
- **Only three members of subfamily B** were identified: PP2C46, PP2C47, and PP2C72
- PP2C46 and PP2C47 are closely related to Arabidopsis AP2C1
- AP2C1 is a well-characterized MAPK phosphatase (interacts with MPK4 and MPK6)

### Clade B Function: MAPK Regulation, NOT ABA Signaling
- AP2C1 (Arabidopsis homolog of MtPP2C46/47) is a negative regulator of stress-induced MAP kinase cascade
- It inactivates MPK4 and MPK6 by dephosphorylation
- This is functionally distinct from Clade A PP2Cs (ABA signaling)
- Clade B PP2Cs are involved in wounding response, pathogen defense, and developmental processes

### PP2C Numbering Convention
- Numbering in Medicago follows sequential system based on chromosomal distribution
- MtPP2C1 through MtPP2C94, arranged by genomic position
- The "47" designation is species-specific — different species' PP2C47 may not be homologous
- However, the clade classification (A, B, C, etc.) is conserved across species

## Critical Implications for xRNA Project
- **This is the strongest available evidence on PP2C47 clade identity**: MtPP2C47 = Clade B = MAPK phosphatase
- If the lettuce PP2C47 target shares the same clade membership (Clade B), then:
  1. Its downregulation would NOT trigger ABA hypersensitivity
  2. It would instead reduce suppression of MAPK signaling
  3. Reduced MAPK suppression could have complex effects on defense and stress responses
  4. The PP2C47 paradox (Contradiction 1) would be RESOLVED
- **CAUTION**: The lettuce PP2C47 numbering may not match Medicago numbering
  - Need BLAST of the actual lettuce gene sequence against the Arabidopsis PP2C phylogeny
  - The gene name "PP2C47" alone is insufficient to determine clade membership across species

## Limitations
- Medicago is a legume (Fabaceae), not Asteraceae — different from lettuce
- Gene numbering is species-specific; MtPP2C47 ≠ lettuce PP2C47 necessarily
- However, if the lettuce gene was annotated based on homology to Medicago or similar databases, the clade assignment may transfer
