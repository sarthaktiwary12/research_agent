## Abstract
Secretory carrier membrane proteins (SCAMPs) are integral membrane proteins found in secretory and endocytic carriers. Their conserved structure consists of a cytoplasmic N-terminal domain with NPF repeats, four transmembrane regions, and a cytoplasmic tail. In plant cells, SCAMP2 localizes to the trans-Golgi, TGN, plasma membrane, and secretory vesicle clusters (SVCs). SVCs are motile structures generated from TGN that move in the cell and eventually fuse with the plasma membrane or cell plate during cytokinesis; they were found in Arabidopsis, rice, and tobacco. The Arabidopsis genome encodes five SCAMP family members. Recent research (2025 preprint) identified three conserved tyrosine motifs in SCAMP5 that assist anterograde transport from TGN to PM, while N-terminal NPF motifs are crucial for endocytosis. SCAMPs dimerize at the plasma membrane and endosomes, and dimerization is required for internalization. Triple and quintuple scamp mutants are more tolerant to drought due to reduced aquaporin (PIP) abundance at the PM in root cells, identifying SCAMPs as regulators of PIP trafficking and water transport.

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** Research Question 2 — SCAMP3 role in vesicle trafficking and aquaporin regulation. SCAMPs control both anterograde (TGN → PM) and endocytic trafficking, and specifically regulate aquaporin (PIP) abundance at the PM. This directly connects to water uptake during radicle protrusion.
- **Partially addresses:** The broader membrane trafficking network — SCAMPs operate at the TGN-PM axis, the same axis controlled by V-ATPase (paper_032) and ROP/exocyst (paper_027).
- **Novel contribution:** The aquaporin trafficking connection is the most important finding for the xRNA research brief. SCAMP downregulation reduces PIP aquaporin abundance at the PM → reduced water permeability → potentially reduced turgor-driven cell expansion. This is a PARADOX for the xRNA hypothesis: if xRNA downregulates SCAMP3 and this reduces aquaporin delivery to the PM, it should SLOW radicle protrusion (less water uptake, less turgor), not accelerate germination. HOWEVER, there are possible resolutions: (1) SCAMP3 may have a specialized role different from SCAMP5; (2) Reduced aquaporin delivery might be compensated by other water uptake mechanisms; (3) The effect may depend on timing — early reduction in water channel density could be overcome by other expansion mechanisms; (4) SCAMP3 downregulation may primarily affect trafficking of OTHER cargo (not aquaporins) in the germination context; (5) If SCAMP3 downregulation alters the balance between secretory and endocytic flux, it could favor net membrane expansion even with reduced aquaporin delivery. This paradox requires careful analysis in the synthesis phase.
