## Abstract
This study demonstrates that exogenous miRNAs can induce post-transcriptional gene silencing (PTGS) in plants. A miR156-dependent root phenotype was observed in plants fed with synthetic miR156, indicating that modulation of SPL genes by exogenous miR156 is strong enough to affect the phenotype of the receiving plant. Exogenous ds-miR156 modulated root architecture by adjusting expression of SPL3. The results demonstrate that exogenous small RNAs can be taken up by plant cells and loaded into the endogenous RISC machinery to silence target genes. miRNAs produced by plants can also act as signaling molecules affecting gene expression in nearby plants, and this RNAi induced by exogenous miRNAs requires the production of secondary siRNAs. This paper provides critical mechanistic support for the concept that exogenous RNA treatment (including tRFs) could modify plant phenotypes relevant to germination and growth.
