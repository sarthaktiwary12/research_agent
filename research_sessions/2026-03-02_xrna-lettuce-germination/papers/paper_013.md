## Abstract
This study demonstrates that exogenous miRNAs can induce post-transcriptional gene silencing (PTGS) in plants. A miR156-dependent root phenotype was observed in plants fed with synthetic miR156, indicating that modulation of SPL genes by exogenous miR156 is strong enough to affect the phenotype of the receiving plant. Exogenous ds-miR156 modulated root architecture by adjusting expression of SPL3. The results demonstrate that exogenous small RNAs can be taken up by plant cells and loaded into the endogenous RISC machinery to silence target genes. miRNAs produced by plants can also act as signaling molecules affecting gene expression in nearby plants, and this RNAi induced by exogenous miRNAs requires the production of secondary siRNAs. This paper provides critical mechanistic support for the concept that exogenous RNA treatment (including tRFs) could modify plant phenotypes relevant to germination and growth.

### Limitations
- **Stated by authors:** Not available from abstract
- **Additional concerns:** (1) miR156 is a 21-nt miRNA with well-characterized targets — this may not directly extrapolate to 16-22 nt tRFs which have different structures (G-quadruplex) and potentially different AGO loading preferences; (2) Root uptake may differ mechanistically from seed coat/germinating embryo uptake relevant to the xRNA lettuce application; (3) ds-miRNA format used here differs from the single-stranded G4-forming tRF in the xRNA drug; (4) Whether secondary siRNA amplification occurs with tRF triggers (which may lack the 22-nt size requirement for RDR6 recruitment) is unknown; (5) No evidence of nucleolin-mediated uptake — the uptake pathway for miR156 feeding is likely different from receptor-mediated endocytosis

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** Core question of whether exogenous small RNAs can silence target genes in plants — yes, with phenotypic consequences; validates the fundamental principle that the xRNA drug could silence germination-related transcripts
- **Partially addresses:** Mechanism of exogenous RNA uptake by plant cells (demonstrates it occurs but does not characterize the receptor/pathway); efficiency of PTGS by exogenous RNAs; signal amplification through secondary siRNAs
- **Novel contribution:** Provides the strongest direct evidence that exogenous small RNA feeding can cause a measurable phenotypic change in plants, which is the core premise of the xRNA lettuce germination application. The secondary siRNA requirement suggests potential signal amplification.
