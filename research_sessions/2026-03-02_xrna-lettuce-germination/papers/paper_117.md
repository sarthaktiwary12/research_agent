## Abstract
Bioactive tRNA fragments (tiRNAs) assemble into intermolecular RNA G-quadruplexes (RG4s). The study provides evidence for the formation of uniquely stable tetramolecular RG4 structures consisting of five tetrad layers. RG4 is required for the regulation of mRNA translation by tiRNAs as a critical component of cellular stress response. RG4 disruption abrogates the ability of tRNA fragments to trigger stress granule formation in vivo. The tetramolecular nature of the G4 assemblies means four individual tiRNA molecules cooperate to form the functional structure.
