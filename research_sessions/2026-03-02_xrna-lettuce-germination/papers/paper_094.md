## Abstract
Noncoding RNAs (ncRNAs), once considered the "dark matter" of the genome, have emerged as critical regulators of gene expression in plants. Research initially focused on model organisms has laid the groundwork for harnessing the potential of ncRNAs in agriculture, particularly for crop protection, improvement, and modulation. The review examines the latest strategies for ncRNA expression and delivery in crops, including transgenic and nontransgenic approaches, as well as emerging technologies that enable precise and efficient modulation of gene activity in plants and pathogens. By leveraging the advances in plant science research, long and small ncRNAs hold promise for designing highly specific tools to boost crop productivity while preserving genetic diversity, contributing to global food security and sustainable agriculture. This includes spray-induced gene silencing (SIGS), nanoparticle-based delivery systems, and cross-kingdom RNAi approaches for crop improvement without genetic modification.
