## Abstract
The folding of tRNA fragments (tRFs) into G-quadruplex structures and the implications of G-quadruplexes in translational inhibition have been studied mainly in mammalian systems. The authors determined the influence of human and plant tRFs and model G-quadruplexes on translation in rabbit reticulocyte lysate and wheat germ extract. Six tRFs identified in Arabidopsis thaliana and human cells were selected, all with 5'-TOG motifs with 3-5 consecutive G residues. The efficiency of translational inhibition in the mammalian system was strongly associated with the type of G-quadruplex topology. However, in the plant system (wheat germ extract), the ability of a small RNA to adopt the G-quadruplex conformation was NOT sufficient to repress translation, indicating the importance of other structural determinants. Results strongly suggest different structural determinants of translational inhibition by tRFs in mammalian and plant systems.
