## Abstract
This review covers the VQ motif-containing protein family, plant-specific transcriptional regulators containing the conserved FxxxVQxhTG motif. VQ proteins interact with WRKY transcription factors (specifically group I and IIc WRKY proteins) to regulate plant growth, development, and defense. VQ22 (JAV1/JASMONATE-ASSOCIATED VQ MOTIF GENE1) is highlighted as a key negative regulator of JA-mediated defense: silencing VQ22 enhanced resistance to necrotrophic pathogens and herbivorous insects, while constitutive VQ22 expression caused high susceptibility. Critically for the defense-growth tradeoff, overexpression of VQ17, VQ18, or VQ22 displayed stunted growth. VQ22 interacts with WRKY28 and WRKY51 to negatively control defense, and JA/wounding triggers VQ22 degradation via the 26S proteasome in a COI1-dependent manner. In soybean, a natural deletion in VQ22 abolishes WRKY binding and was positively selected during domestication for improved growth traits. AtVQ18 and AtVQ26 function as negative regulators of ABA-induced signaling through interaction with ABI5 during seed germination. This family represents a critical molecular node where defense and growth signals are integrated.
