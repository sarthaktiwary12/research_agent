# Integration of ABA, GA, and light signaling in seed germination through the regulation of ABI5 — Role of E3 ubiquitin ligases

## Abstract
Seed germination is controlled by light signals and the phytohormones abscisic acid (ABA) and gibberellin (GA), which play antagonistic roles. The ubiquitin-proteasome system (UPS) is central to regulating ABA signaling during germination. Multiple E3 ubiquitin ligases target key signaling components for proteasomal degradation: PUB8 (U-box E3 ligase) physically interacts with ABI3 and ABI5, promoting their 26S proteasome-mediated degradation to attenuate ABA responses during early seedling growth. PUB41 positively regulates ABA signaling affecting seed dormancy and drought response. KEG (KEEP ON GOING, RING-type E3 ligase) mediates cytoplasmic degradation of ABI5. AIP2 targets ABI3 for degradation. CER9 (putative E3 ligase) negatively regulates ABA biosynthesis during seed germination. RHA2a positively regulates ABA-mediated germination control independently of ABI3/ABI4/ABI5. The GA receptor GID1 promotes SCF E3 ligase SLY1-mediated degradation of DELLA repressors (RGA, RGL2) to release GA signaling. ABI5 is a central regulatory hub where multiple E3 ligases converge, and its transcriptional activity is activated through SnRK2-mediated phosphorylation. About 5% of the Arabidopsis genome (>1400 genes) encodes UPS components, reflecting the pivotal role of ubiquitination in proteome homeostasis during germination.

## Deep Reading Notes

### Key Findings
- ABI5 (ABSCISIC ACID INSENSITIVE 5) is the central transcription factor hub where ABA, GA, and light signaling converge to regulate seed germination
- Multiple E3 ubiquitin ligases target ABI5 for proteasomal degradation, constituting a multi-layered regulatory system:
  - **KEG** (RING-type E3): Mediates cytoplasmic ABI5 degradation; ABA induces KEG self-ubiquitination and destruction, stabilizing ABI5
  - **PUB8** (U-box E3): Physically interacts with ABI3 and ABI5; promotes their 26S proteasome-mediated degradation to attenuate ABA responses during early seedling growth. Loss-of-function pub8 mutants are ABA-hypersensitive; overexpression reduces ABI5 protein
  - **PUB35** (U-box E3): Specifically targets ABI5 and AFP1 (but not homologs)
  - **CUL4-DDB1^DWA1/2**: Nuclear degradation of ABI5 via DWA1/DWA2 substrate receptors
  - **CUL4-DDB1^ABD1**: Nuclear ABI5 degradation; COP1 ubiquitinates ABD1 to stabilize ABI5 in darkness
  - **AIP2**: Targets ABI3 for degradation
  - **CER9**: Negatively regulates ABA biosynthesis during seed germination
  - **MIEL1** (RING-type E3): Also targets ABI5
- PUB41 positively regulates ABA signaling affecting seed dormancy and drought response
- RHA2a positively regulates ABA-mediated germination control independently of ABI3/ABI4/ABI5
- GA promotes degradation of DELLA repressors (RGA, RGL2) through GID1 receptor and SLY1 E3 ligase
- DELLA protein RGL2 enhances ABI5 expression by interacting with NF-YC transcription factors and binding the ABI5 promoter
- COP1 links light and ABA signaling: in darkness, COP1 stabilizes ABI5 by degrading ABD1
- ABI5 transcriptional activity is activated through SnRK2-mediated phosphorylation
- About 5% of the Arabidopsis genome (>1400 genes) encodes UPS components

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** Research question 1 — "How does simultaneous downregulation of PP2C47, UVR8, and VQ22 shift ABA/GA balance?" This paper provides the molecular framework for understanding how E3 ubiquitin ligases regulate the ABA-GA balance through ABI5 stability control. If an E3 ligase that degrades ABI5 is downregulated by xRNA, ABI5 would accumulate, STRENGTHENING ABA signaling and potentially INHIBITING germination. Conversely, if an E3 ligase that promotes ABA biosynthesis (like the reverse of CER9) is downregulated, ABA levels would decrease, promoting germination.
- **Partially addresses:** Research question 7 (Dominant Theme Synthesis) — UPS-mediated hormone signaling is a major regulatory mechanism
- **Novel contribution:** The key mechanistic insight is that ABI5 is a convergence hub for ABA, GA, and light signaling, and its protein stability is controlled by at least 6+ E3 ligases operating in different subcellular compartments. This creates both redundancy and specificity. For the xRNA hypothesis, the DIRECTION of the effect (pro- or anti-germination) depends entirely on WHICH E3 ligase is downregulated:
  - Downregulating KEG, PUB8, PUB35, DWA1/2, or ABD1 → ABI5 accumulation → enhanced ABA response → INHIBITED germination
  - Downregulating RHA2a or PUB41 → reduced ABA signaling → PROMOTED germination
  - Downregulating SLY1 → DELLA accumulation → enhanced ABA signaling via RGL2-ABI5 → INHIBITED germination
