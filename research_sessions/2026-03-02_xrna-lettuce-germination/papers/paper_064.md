## Abstract
Protein S-acylation (palmitoylation) is a reversible post-translational lipid modification in which a 16-carbon palmitate attaches to cysteine residues via a thioester bond. The status of S-acylation is dynamic and reversible, catalyzed by protein S-acyltransferases (PATs) and reversed by acyl protein thioesterases. PATs feature a highly conserved DHHC cysteine-rich domain. In Arabidopsis, at least 24 PATs exist with complex cellular membrane localization: half reside in the plasma membrane (the main palmitoylation site in plants, unlike mammals where Golgi is primary) and others in the ER, Golgi, vesicles, and tonoplast. The S-acylation/de-S-acylation cycle provides a molecular switch for membrane-associated protein cycling between cell compartments. S-acylation plays critical roles in plant development and stress responses, including cell polarization, expansion, cytoskeletal organization, hormone signaling, and disease resistance. Key examples: AtPAT14 regulates leaf senescence via salicylic acid; PAT19/20/22 mediate brassinosteroid signaling through BSK1 S-acylation; MdPAT16 in apple regulates salt stress tolerance. Thousands of plant proteins have been identified as S-acylated through proteomics.
