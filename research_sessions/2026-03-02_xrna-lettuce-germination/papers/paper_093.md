## Abstract
Seeds preserve a far-developed plant embryo in a quiescent state. Seed metabolism relies on stored resources and is reactivated to drive germination when external conditions are favorable. Central components and processes of the mitochondrial respiratory metabolism were enriched, including those of the mitochondrial respiratory chain and the TCA cycle, suggesting central respiratory metabolism harbors hotspots of operational thiol switching. Fasting Drosophila and quiescent plant mitochondria may have in common that metabolic flux is insufficient to provide enough NADPH to operate the thiol redox machinery effectively. By providing substrate to mitochondrial metabolism, the degree of Cys oxidation is effectively lowered, reaching comparable levels to the in vivo values of metabolically active organisms. The authors conclude that redox regulation contributes to efficient metabolism during early seed germination. This makes Cys-based redox regulation of chloroplast metabolism a specific case of a more fundamental principle of regulation during metabolic transitions.
