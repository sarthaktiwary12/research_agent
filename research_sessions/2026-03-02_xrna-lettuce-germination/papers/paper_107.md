## Abstract
Endosperm cap (CAP) weakening and embryo elongation growth are prerequisites for lettuce seed germination. While cell wall loosening has been proposed to result from enzymatic mechanisms, it remains unclear which enzymes are involved. This study shows that reactive oxygen species (ROS), which are non-enzymatic factors, are involved. The amount of O2·- and H2O2 and the activity of peroxidase in the CAP of Guasihong lettuce seeds increased during imbibition, coinciding with decreased CAP puncture force. ROS scavengers and inhibitors reduced CAP weakening and germination percentage. Exogenous ROS and promoters increased CAP rupture. H2O2 addition recovered germination in seeds treated with the ROS inhibitor SDIC.
