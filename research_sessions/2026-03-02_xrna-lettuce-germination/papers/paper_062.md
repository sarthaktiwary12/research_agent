## Abstract
Phosphatidylinositol monophosphate 5-kinase (PIP5K) catalyzes the synthesis of PI-4,5-bisphosphate (PtdIns(4,5)P2) by phosphorylation of PI-4-phosphate at the 5 position of the inositol ring and regulates multiple developmental processes and stress responses. All PIP5K proteins contain a conserved catalytic domain (PIPKc) at the C-terminus and a large N-terminal extension characterized by membrane occupation and recognition nexus (MORN) repeats unique to plant PIP5K families. In Arabidopsis, PIP5K2 is expressed during lateral root initiation and elongation, and the pip5k2 knockout shows reduced lateral root formation and delayed root gravity response due to decelerated vesicle trafficking and suppressed cycling of PIN proteins. PIP5K3 localizes to the plasma membrane of elongating root hair apices and is involved in root hair tip growth, regulating actin dynamics through profilin interaction. PIP5K3 specialized for root hair elongation, whereas PIP5K1 differentiated from PIP5K2 only in its promoter-directed expression pattern.
