## Abstract
In Arabidopsis, stress-induced tDR-Ala-5D (a 5' tRNA-Ala-derived fragment) directly interacts with the mRNA splicing factor SERINE-ARGININE RICH PROTEIN 34 (SR34). The binding affinity of SR34 to target transcripts was lower in the presence of tDR-Ala-5D, as tDR-Ala-5D effectively competes with SR34 for binding to RNA targets with cognate motifs. This demonstrates that stress-induced tRFs can interfere with SR34 binding and alter alternative splicing processes. tDR-Ala-5D also interacts with eIF4E1, suggesting dual roles in both translation and splicing regulation. Gene ontology enrichment shows candidate interacting proteins are associated with stress response, DNA/RNA metabolism, transport, and energy metabolism.
