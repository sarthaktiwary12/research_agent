## Abstract
This comprehensive review covers the thermoinhibition of lettuce seed germination — the inability of seeds to germinate at temperatures above 28-30 degrees C. Key factors reviewed include: (1) Endosperm hardening as a physical barrier; (2) The role of ABA, whose biosynthesis increases at high temperatures (through activation of NCED genes) and whose catabolism is repressed (CYP707A genes); (3) GA's ability to promote ABA catabolism — at 33 degrees C, exogenous GA3 promotes ABA decomposition; (4) Ethylene's promotion of germination under thermoinhibition primarily by increasing endo-beta-mannanase activity; (5) The LsNCED4 gene identified by QTL analysis (Htg6.1 locus) as the causal gene for thermoinhibition sensitivity; (6) Light and temperature responses rely on antagonistic regulation of ABA and GA metabolism. Low-concentration ABA receptor antagonist (ANT) treatments (10 uM) during imbibition reduce thermoinhibition at temperatures up to 40 degrees C. The review concludes that with global warming, understanding and overcoming thermoinhibition is increasingly important for lettuce production.

### CRITICAL ANALYSIS: Implications for PP2C47 Targeting in Lettuce

**This review establishes that lettuce thermoinhibition is FUNDAMENTALLY an ABA-driven process.** The key molecular events are:

1. High temperature → LsNCED4 upregulation → ABA biosynthesis increases
2. High temperature → CYP707A suppression → ABA catabolism decreases
3. Net effect: ABA accumulates → seeds fail to germinate

**For PP2C47 targeting:** If PP2C47 is a clade A PP2C in lettuce:
- PP2C47 downregulation → enhanced ABA signaling → seeds become MORE sensitive to the ABA that accumulates during thermoinhibition → germination is FURTHER inhibited
- This is especially problematic under the heat stress conditions where lettuce germination uniformity matters most

**Alternatively, if PP2C47 acts in a context-dependent manner:**
- Under NORMAL (cool) temperatures where ABA is low, PP2C downregulation may have minimal effect because the ABA-PYR/PYL-PP2C-SnRK2 module is largely inactive
- The observed germination acceleration by xRNA could come from OTHER targets in the 70-transcript panel (e.g., defense genes, UVR8, VQ22) rather than from PP2C47

**Key insight from this review:** The ABA-GA antagonism is BIDIRECTIONAL — GA promotes ABA catabolism. If other xRNA targets shift GA-ABA balance toward GA (e.g., by reducing GA catabolism or ABA biosynthesis indirectly), this could mask the PP2C47 pro-dormancy effect.
