# The Universally Conserved HCF101 Protein Is Involved in Assembly of [4Fe-4S]-Cluster-Containing Complexes in Arabidopsis thaliana Chloroplasts

- **Year:** 2004
- **Source:** The Plant Journal

- **Citation Count:** N/A
- **Source Database:** Cluster B — PubMed (WebSearch)

## Abstract
The seedling-lethal nuclear Arabidopsis hcf101 (high chlorophyll fluorescence) mutant is impaired in photosynthesis. Photosystem I (PSI) activity is abolished, and PSI core complexes fail to accumulate in hcf101, whereas levels of other thylakoid membrane proteins are unaffected. PSI transcript levels and translation rates of PSI proteins are normal. Mutant plants not only fail to accumulate mature PSI, which contains three [4Fe-4S] clusters (FSCs), but also have reduced levels of the soluble FSC-containing complex ferredoxin-thioredoxin reductase (FTR) in the stroma. Levels of the [2Fe-2S] cluster-containing proteins ferredoxin and PetC were unchanged, suggesting a specific role of HCF101 in [4Fe-4S] cluster biogenesis. HCF101 is plastid localized and belongs to an ancient and universally conserved family of P-loop ATPases.

### Key Findings
- The hcf101 mutant is seedling-lethal, demonstrating that HCF101 is essential for seedling viability in Arabidopsis
- PSI activity is completely abolished in hcf101 mutants; PSI core complexes fail to accumulate
- Other thylakoid membrane proteins (PSII, cytochrome b6f, etc.) are unaffected, showing specificity of HCF101 for [4Fe-4S]-containing complexes
- PSI transcript levels and PSI protein translation rates are normal, indicating the defect is post-translational (at the level of cofactor insertion/complex assembly)
- Ferredoxin-thioredoxin reductase (FTR), a soluble [4Fe-4S] protein, is also reduced, confirming HCF101 acts broadly on [4Fe-4S] cluster biogenesis
- [2Fe-2S] proteins (ferredoxin, PetC/Rieske) are unaffected, demonstrating specificity for [4Fe-4S] vs. [2Fe-2S] cluster types
- HCF101 is plastid-localized and belongs to the P-loop ATPase superfamily, suggesting ATP-dependent Fe-S cluster transfer

### Limitations
- **Stated by authors:** Not assessable from abstract
- **Additional concerns:** (1) Study is in Arabidopsis; lettuce HCF101 ortholog function must be inferred. (2) The seedling-lethal phenotype represents a complete loss of function — partial downregulation (as by xRNA) could have different, graded effects. (3) No analysis of the transition from dark-grown to light-grown seedlings, which is the most relevant timeframe for our germination question. (4) Does not address whether partial HCF101 reduction might be beneficial by slowing premature PSI assembly.

### Relevance to Research Brief
- **Directly addresses:** Research question 5 — HCF101's role in PSI assembly and Fe-S cluster biogenesis in chloroplasts. Provides the mechanistic foundation for predicting what HCF101 downregulation would do during germination.
- **Partially addresses:** The "organelle & energy tuning" module broadly; the FTR finding connects HCF101 to the ferredoxin-thioredoxin redox regulatory system.
- **Novel contribution:** Establishes that HCF101 is THE bottleneck for [4Fe-4S] cluster insertion into PSI. This means partial downregulation would specifically slow PSI maturation without affecting PSII, cytochrome b6f, or [2Fe-2S] ferredoxin. This is critical: it predicts that xRNA-mediated HCF101 downregulation would delay the assembly of the PSI acceptor side (FA, FB clusters) and FTR, potentially reducing ROS generation from premature electron transfer to O2 when PSI lacks proper acceptors during the dark-to-light transition of germination.
