## Abstract
Flavin-containing monooxygenases (FMOs) in plants catalyze specific steps in the biosynthesis of auxin or in the metabolism of glucosinolates, and have roles in pathogen defense. Glucosinolates are amino acid-derived secondary metabolites in Brassicaceae with diverse biological activities in biotic defense. The basic methylthioalkyl (MT) glucosinolates undergo S-oxygenation to form methylsulfinylalkyl (MS) glucosinolates, which influences the biosynthesis of MS GSLs and the activity of their hydrolysis products (isothiocyanates and nitriles). Five FMOs (FMOGS-OX1-5) were previously identified for this S-oxygenation step. This study identifies At1g12130 (FMOGS-OX6) and At1g12160 (FMOGS-OX7) as two additional FMOs involved in S-oxygenation of aliphatic glucosinolates, while At1g12200 lacks expected activity. Phylogenetically, plant FMOs divide into Clade I (pathogen defense), Clade II (YUCCA, auxin biosynthesis), and Clade III (S-oxygenating/glucosinolate). Upon tissue damage, glucosinolates are hydrolyzed by myrosinases producing isothiocyanates and nitriles that function as anti-microbials, feeding deterrents for herbivores, and attractants for specialist herbivores.

### Key Findings
- Flavin-containing monooxygenases (FMOs) in plants catalyze specific steps in auxin biosynthesis (Clade II/YUCCA) or glucosinolate metabolism (Clade III/GS-OX)
- FMO GS-OX enzymes catalyze the S-oxygenation of methylthioalkyl (MT) glucosinolates to methylsulfinylalkyl (MS) glucosinolates — a key structural modification step in aliphatic glucosinolate biosynthesis
- Seven FMO GS-OX enzymes now identified: FMOGS-OX1-5 (previously known) plus FMOGS-OX6 (At1g12130) and FMOGS-OX7 (At1g12160) newly identified in this study; At1g12200 lacks expected activity
- S-oxygenation influences both the biosynthesis of MS glucosinolates and the activity of their hydrolysis products (isothiocyanates and nitriles)
- Plant FMOs divide phylogenetically into three clades: Clade I (pathogen defense), Clade II (YUCCA/auxin biosynthesis), and Clade III (S-oxygenating/glucosinolate)
- Upon tissue damage, glucosinolates are hydrolyzed by myrosinases producing isothiocyanates (antimicrobial, herbivore deterrent) and nitriles
- Glucosinolates are amino acid-derived secondary metabolites specific to Brassicales — lettuce (Asteraceae) does NOT produce glucosinolates
- All seven FMOGS-OX genes show responsive expression to ABA, ACC, JA, SA, IAA, and temperature changes, but with varying sensitivities

### Metabolic Cost Analysis (Key for Research Brief)
- **Glucosinolate biosynthesis cost:** Glucosinolate production is a resource-demanding process involving:
  - Amino acid precursors (methionine for aliphatic glucosinolates; tryptophan for indolic)
  - Multiple enzymatic steps requiring NADPH, sulfur donors, and UDP-glucose
  - S-oxygenation step requires NADPH and FAD cofactors
  - The "mustard oil bomb" defense system requires both glucosinolate synthesis AND myrosinase expression
- **Growth-defense trade-off:** Recent literature (Jeschke et al., 2022) establishes that glucosinolates and their catabolites mediate growth-defense trade-offs, with some catabolites also having growth-regulatory functions
- **Critical caveat for lettuce:** Glucosinolates are ABSENT from lettuce (Asteraceae). If the xRNA target is an FMO in lettuce, it would not be a glucosinolate-pathway FMO. Lettuce FMOs would more likely be involved in:
  - Auxin biosynthesis (YUCCA-type, Clade II)
  - Other defense compound modifications
  - The relevance of this paper to the lettuce xRNA hypothesis depends on whether the predicted "FMO GS-OX" target is actually a GS-OX ortholog or a different FMO family member

### Limitations
