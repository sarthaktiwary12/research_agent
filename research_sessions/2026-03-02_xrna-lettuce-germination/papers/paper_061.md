# Arabidopsis tRNA-derived fragments as potential modulators of translation

## Abstract
Both tRFs purified from Arabidopsis thaliana leaves and specific synthetic tRFs deriving from the 5' extremity of Arabidopsis tRNA-Ala and tRNA-Asn efficiently inhibit protein synthesis in vitro. This inhibition is cap-independent and does not require sequence complementarity with the mRNA to be translated. Analysis of Arabidopsis ribosomal fractions shows that tRFs can bind and be associated with heavy polysomes, suggesting their involvement in global regulation of the translation process. Unlike the mechanism in humans, the G18 and G19 residues of Arabidopsis tRF-Ala, but not the 4 Gs present at the 5' ends, are essential for translation inhibition. The 5' tRF-Ala-AGC or 5' tRF-Asn-GTT needs to associate with polyribosomes to induce translation inhibition, suggesting that tRFs may act as general modulation factors of the translation process in plants.
