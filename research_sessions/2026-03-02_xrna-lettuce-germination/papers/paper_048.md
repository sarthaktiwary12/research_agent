## Abstract
Many plant lncRNAs regulate gene expression by binding to chromatin, but how they are retained at the target loci is unclear. The authors identify a new chromatin-localized lncRNA — MUSHER — which activates two parallel regulatory pathways to increase Arabidopsis seed dormancy. Mechanistically, both MUSHER targets (PIR1 and DOG1) interact with PP2CA protein, a regulator of ABA signalling. PIR1 binding to PP2CA promotes its degradation, while DOG1 inhibits PP2CA phosphatase activity. The study reveals how a single lncRNA integrates ABA and DOG1 signaling pathways through convergent regulation of PP2C phosphatase to control seed dormancy.

## Key Findings
- MUSHER lncRNA is chromatin-localized and activates two parallel pathways (PIR1 and DOG1) to increase seed dormancy
- Both PIR1 and DOG1 converge on PP2CA (a clade A PP2C phosphatase)
- PIR1 promotes PP2CA protein degradation
- DOG1 inhibits PP2CA phosphatase activity
- Demonstrates PP2C as a central integration node for ABA and DOG1 dormancy signaling
