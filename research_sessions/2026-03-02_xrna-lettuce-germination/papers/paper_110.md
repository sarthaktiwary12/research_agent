## Abstract
Systemic signalling in plants entails the activation of Ca2+-permeable GLR channels by wound-released glutamate. This study reveals a calmodulin-mediated, Ca2+-dependent desensitization of GLR channels — a negative feedback loop in the orchestration of plant systemic wound responses. The desensitization mechanism shapes the temporal dynamics of calcium waves, controlling the amplitude and duration of systemic wound signaling and consequent jasmonate-mediated anti-herbivore defence.
