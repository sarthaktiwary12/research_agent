## Abstract
This study measured the neurochemical effects of acute ketosis on the human brain using ultra-high-field 1H MR Spectroscopy (MRS) and investigated the impact on neural function through resting-state fMRI. In a within-subjects design, N = 63 healthy adults from across the lifespan underwent 1H MRS and rsfMRI scans before and after consuming individually weight-dosed and calorically-matched ketone monoester or glucose drinks.

Ketone monoester administration significantly elevated cerebral antioxidants and energy markers while decreasing GABA, glutamate, and glutamine levels in the posterior cingulate cortex (PCC). Notably, increased bioenergetics, specifically an increase in total creatine, correlated with greater improvements in neural function.

This study is relevant to the creatine-cognition link because it provides direct in vivo evidence in healthy human adults that brain total creatine increases correlate with neural function improvements, supporting the bioenergetic mechanism hypothesis for creatine's cognitive effects.
