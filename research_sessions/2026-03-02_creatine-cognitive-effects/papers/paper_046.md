## Abstract
Creatine (Cr) is a nitrogenous organic acid that plays roles as a fast phosphate energy buffer to replenish ATP, osmolyte, antioxidant, neuromodulator, and as a compound with anabolic and ergogenic properties in muscle. Loss-of-function mutations in the genes encoding for the creatine synthesis enzymes or the creatine transporter cause Cerebral Creatine Deficiency Syndromes (CCDS), characterized by brain creatine deficiency, intellectual disability with severe speech delay, behavioral troubles, epilepsy, and motor dysfunction.

Among CCDS, the X-linked creatine transporter deficiency (CTD) is the most prevalent with no efficient treatment so far. This study describes a rat model of CTD showing motor dysfunction, cerebellar alterations, and muscle creatine deficiency.

Note: This is an animal model study focused on creatine transporter deficiency. While not directly about supplementation in healthy adults, it provides essential mechanistic context for understanding why brain creatine matters for cognitive function — when creatine cannot reach the brain, intellectual disability results.
