## Abstract
This systematic review of randomized controlled trials evaluated the effects of creatine supplementation on cognitive function in healthy individuals. The review examined evidence across multiple cognitive domains including short-term memory, intelligence/reasoning, and other measures. The authors found evidence that short-term memory and intelligence/reasoning may be improved by creatine administration. The review highlighted the role of the creatine kinase-phosphocreatine (CK-PCr) energy buffer system in maintaining brain energy supply, noting the presence of creatine kinase isoforms in multiple brain areas including the cerebellum, hippocampus, pontine reticular formation, red nucleus, cerebral cortex, and choroid plexus. The paper discussed how phosphocreatine and ADP are converted to creatine and ATP in a reversible reaction catalyzed by creatine kinase, occurring faster than oxidative phosphorylation and glycolytic processes.
