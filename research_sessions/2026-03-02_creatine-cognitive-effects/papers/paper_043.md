## Abstract
Creatine transporter deficiency (CTD) prevents creatine uptake into the brain, leading to mental retardation. To better understand the pathophysiology and identify potential treatments, this study focuses on the identification of biomarkers related to cognitive improvement in a Slc6a8 knockout mouse model of CTD. Treatment with dodecyl creatine ester (DCE), a creatine prodrug that bypasses the transporter, was evaluated.

Shotgun proteomics analysis of 4,035 proteins in four different brain regions was performed in 24 mice. Comparisons identified 14 biomarkers shedding light on the mechanism of action of DCE, and integrative bioinformatics identified key proteins associated with CTD, including KIF1A and PLCB1.

Note: This is an animal model study (mice), not a human supplementation trial. However, it provides mechanistic insight into how brain creatine levels affect cognitive function and identifies molecular pathways through which creatine restoration improves cognition.
