## Abstract
Researchers reproducibly detected creatine (Cr) in synaptic vesicles (SVs). Creatine has long been considered as an energy buffer in muscle and brain. This study provides evidence for creatine's presence in synaptic vesicles, its release upon stimulation, and its effects on cortical neurons — suggesting it may function as a central neurotransmitter. This is a fundamentally new perspective on creatine's role in the brain beyond its established function in the phosphocreatine/creatine kinase energy buffering system.
