## Abstract
This study used a preregistered, cross-over, double-blind, placebo-controlled, and randomised design to evaluate the effects of creatine supplementation on cognitive performance. Participants received daily supplementation of 5 g creatine monohydrate for 6 weeks each in a crossover design. This represents one of the largest and most rigorously designed studies to date examining creatine's cognitive effects. The study assessed multiple cognitive domains including processing speed, episodic memory, and attention. The findings contribute to the understanding of creatine's effects on cognition in healthy adults under standard (non-stressed) conditions. The rigorous crossover design with adequate washout period addresses many methodological limitations of prior studies. Since creatine does not cross the blood-brain barrier readily, the 6-week supplementation period was chosen to allow sufficient time for brain creatine accumulation. The study provides important data on the dose (5 g/day) and duration (6 weeks) parameters for future research protocols investigating creatine and cognition.
