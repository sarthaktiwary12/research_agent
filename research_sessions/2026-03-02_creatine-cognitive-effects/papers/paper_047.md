## Abstract
Creatine monohydrate (CM) is currently being used as a treatment or adjuvant for neurological conditions using doses as high as 20 g/day. As higher doses of CM are employed, tolerability becomes an issue and can impact compliance. This study examined gastrointestinal and fluid retention symptoms associated with creatine monohydrate supplementation with and without a loading dose over 28 days.

The study references key literature on creatine and brain function, including:
- Fabiano N, Candow D. "Creatine supplementation: More is likely better for brain bioenergetics, health and function."
- A pilot study on "Creatine monohydrate pilot in Alzheimer's: Feasibility, brain creatine and cognition."

This paper is relevant to the creatine-cognition research question because it addresses the practical tolerability of doses needed to meaningfully increase brain creatine, which is a rate-limiting factor for cognitive effects. Side effects were reported significantly more often for creatine than for placebo in the cognitive RCT (paper_040), and understanding the dose-tolerability relationship is critical for designing future cognitive trials.
