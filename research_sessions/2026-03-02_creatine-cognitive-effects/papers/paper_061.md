## Abstract
Although research on creatine has traditionally investigated its role in skeletal and muscle energetics, a growing body of evidence now supports its mechanistic potential to exert therapeutic and neuroprotective effects in brain health. The brain requires a high amount of energy for cellular processes such as neurotransmitter exocytosis and synaptic functioning. The creatine kinase-phosphocreatine system plays a crucial role in maintaining energy supply in the brain. Creatine supplementation can increase brain creatine stores, which may help explain some of the positive effects on measures of cognition and memory, especially in aging adults or during times of metabolic stress (e.g., sleep deprivation). Furthermore, creatine has shown promise for improving health outcome measures associated with muscular dystrophy, traumatic brain injury (including concussions in children), depression, and anxiety. This narrative review provides an overview of creatine's potential applications for brain health and function, covering mechanisms of brain creatine metabolism, effects on cognition, neuroprotection, and therapeutic applications in neurological and psychiatric conditions.
