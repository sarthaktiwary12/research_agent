# Research Brief

## Original Prompt
What is the current scientific evidence on the cognitive effects of creatine supplementation in healthy adults?

## Domain
Science / Neuroscience / Nutrition

## Profile
fast

## Core Research Questions
1. Does creatine supplementation improve cognitive performance in healthy adults?
2. Which cognitive domains (memory, executive function, processing speed, attention) are most affected?
3. What dosing protocols have been studied, and do they differ in effectiveness?
4. What are the proposed neurobiological mechanisms by which creatine could enhance cognition?
5. Are there safety concerns or adverse effects of creatine supplementation on brain function?

## Key Concepts
- **Creatine monohydrate**: The most common supplemental form, widely studied for ergogenic effects
- **Phosphocreatine (PCr)**: The phosphorylated form that serves as a rapid ATP regeneration buffer in the brain
- **Brain bioenergetics**: The energy metabolism of neural tissue, relevant to cognitive demand
- **Cognitive enhancement**: Improvement in measurable cognitive performance metrics
- **Nootropic**: Substances claimed to improve cognitive function

## Scope Boundaries
- **In scope:** Human studies on oral creatine supplementation and cognitive outcomes in healthy adults; mechanistic studies on brain creatine metabolism; meta-analyses and systematic reviews; safety data related to neurological outcomes
- **Out of scope:** Creatine for athletic/muscular performance only; creatine in clinical populations (TBI, depression, neurodegeneration) unless comparing to healthy controls; creatine synthesis deficiency syndromes; non-oral delivery routes
